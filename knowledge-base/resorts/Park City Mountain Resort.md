# Resort Profile: Park City Mountain Resort

## Overview
- **Location:** Park City, Utah
- **Summit Elevation:** 10,026 ft
- **Base Elevation:** 6,800 ft
- **Vertical Drop:** 3,226 ft
- **Annual Snowfall:** 355 inches
- **Skiable Acres:** 7,300 acres
- **State:** Utah

Park City Mountain Resort (PCMR) is the largest ski resort in the United States by skiable acreage and one of the most important venues in US competitive snowboarding and freeskiing. Located in the Wasatch Range 30 miles east of Salt Lake City, PCMR serves as the primary training base and home mountain for the US Ski & Snowboard High Performance Center — the organizational hub of the national team's park and pipe programs. The resort hosts the US Grand Prix competition series, one of the Olympic qualifying events for snowboard and freeski athletes, and operates the most-used competitive halfpipe and slopestyle venues in the country outside of the X Games.

## Competition Infrastructure
Park City Mountain Resort hosts and supports several major events:
- **US Grand Prix** — Annual Olympic qualifier event featuring halfpipe and slopestyle snowboard and freeski disciplines. The resort builds competition-grade features meeting FIS specifications for this event each winter season.
- **US Ski & Snowboard Team Training Facility** — Year-round access to FIS-standard superpipe and slopestyle course. The High Performance Center coordinates athlete access, coaching sessions, and equipment testing on PCMR's terrain park infrastructure.
- **Big Air Jump** — A dedicated outrun-equipped big air jump maintained for US team training in snowboard and freeski big air disciplines.

## Notable Athletes Who Train Here
Park City Mountain Resort serves as the primary or secondary training base for many members of the US Ski & Snowboard team:
- **Shaun White** — Longtime training base for halfpipe snowboard Olympic preparation cycles
- **Alex Hall** — Park City native; primary home resort for freeski slopestyle training
- **Kyle Mack** — Primary big air training base
- **Tommy Ford** — US alpine GS training base
- **Ted Ligety** — Primary GS training base throughout his career (now retired)
- **Red Gerard** — Secondary training base for slopestyle and big air development
- **Hailey Langland** — Slopestyle and big air training base

## Terrain Park & Pipe Specs
- **Olympic Superpipe:** 22-foot halfpipe walls, 600-foot length, maintained to FIS World Cup specifications during competition season
- **Slopestyle Course:** Multiple configuration options to replicate international competition course layouts; up to six rail features and three jump features
- **Big Air Kicker:** Adjustable takeoff angle; output for up to 75-foot amplitude runs
- **Park Features:** Over 150 features across multiple terrain park zones in the resort's general riding areas

## Hosting History
PCMR has hosted US Grand Prix events continuously since the late 1990s and has been the primary domestic qualifier for Olympic pipe and park teams through multiple Olympic cycles. The resort's partnership with US Ski & Snowboard and its proximity to Salt Lake City airport (30-minute drive) make it the most operationally practical high-altitude training venue in the country.
