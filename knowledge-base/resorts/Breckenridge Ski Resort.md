# Resort Profile: Breckenridge Ski Resort

## Overview
- **Location:** Breckenridge, Colorado
- **Summit Elevation:** 12,998 ft
- **Base Elevation:** 9,600 ft
- **Vertical Drop:** 3,398 ft
- **Annual Snowfall:** 360 inches
- **Skiable Acres:** 2,908 acres
- **State:** Colorado

Breckenridge Ski Resort is one of the most visited ski resorts in the United States and a historically significant venue in competitive snowboarding and terrain park development. Located in Summit County, Colorado — approximately 80 miles west of Denver — it operates at one of the highest base elevations in the country, providing high-altitude training conditions that are particularly valuable for competitive athletes. Breckenridge is the home mountain of several prominent US snowboarders, most notably Red Gerard and Chris Corning, who grew up in the nearby town of Silverthorne and trained at Breckenridge throughout their developmental years. The resort has hosted Dew Tour events and US qualifier competitions.

## Competition Infrastructure
- **Dew Tour** — Breckenridge has hosted Dew Tour snowboard and freeski events, featuring halfpipe, slopestyle, and big air disciplines.
- **Terrain Park Competition Features** — The resort maintains competition-standard slopestyle and jump features used for US team training and smaller qualifier events.
- **US Grand Prix Qualifier** — Has served as a venue for US Olympic qualifying competitions at various points in the circuit calendar.

## Notable Athletes Who Train Here
- **Red Gerard** — Grew up in nearby Silverthorne; primary home and training resort throughout his career; won Olympic gold while based here
- **Chris Corning** — Grew up in Silverthorne alongside Gerard; primary home resort and big air/slopestyle training base
- **Shaun White** — Occasional training visits; historical Colorado training base
- **Kyle Mack** — Visits Breckenridge for slopestyle feature training

## Terrain Park & Pipe Specs
- **Independence SuperPipe:** 22-foot walls, 600-foot length; maintained to FIS specifications and used for both athlete training and resort guest riding
- **Freeway Terrain Park:** The largest terrain park zone at Breckenridge; multiple jump and rail features across a long top-to-bottom run
- **Aux Park:** Secondary terrain park with specific features targeted at developing riders
- **Elevation Advantage:** At 12,998 ft summit and 9,600 ft base, Breckenridge provides high-altitude physical training conditions that benefit athletes preparing for high-altitude competition venues

## Hosting History
Breckenridge has played an important role in Colorado's competitive snowboarding infrastructure since the early terrain park era of the 1990s. The resort's consistent investment in park and pipe infrastructure has made it the de facto home mountain for Summit County's community of professional snowboarders — a community that has produced some of the sport's biggest names. The 2018 Pyeongchang Winter Olympics, which produced gold medals for Red Gerard and Chloe Kim, were largely prepared for in the Colorado mountains including at Breckenridge and Copper Mountain.
