# Resort Profile: Copper Mountain

## Overview
- **Location:** Frisco, Colorado
- **Summit Elevation:** 12,313 ft
- **Base Elevation:** 9,712 ft
- **Vertical Drop:** 2,601 ft
- **Annual Snowfall:** 305 inches
- **Skiable Acres:** 2,465 acres
- **State:** Colorado

Copper Mountain is the permanent home venue of the Dew Tour, one of the three most significant competitive snowboard and freeski events in the US alongside X Games Aspen and the Winter Olympics. The resort has invested heavily in competition infrastructure and terrain park development, and its partnership with the Dew Tour has produced annual events that serve as important FIS World Cup qualifying competitions and athlete ranking opportunities. Copper Mountain is also a primary pre-season training venue for US Ski & Snowboard park and pipe athletes and is where many US team athletes complete their fall halfpipe and slopestyle preparation before the competitive season fully begins.

## Competition Infrastructure
- **Dew Tour** — Copper Mountain has hosted the Dew Tour snowboard and freeski event annually. The event features halfpipe, slopestyle, and street/rail disciplines in a format that blends FIS World Cup qualification points with a distinctive competition culture. The Dew Tour at Copper Mountain is held in December, making it one of the season-opening elite events.
- **US Ski & Snowboard Pre-Season Training** — Copper Mountain's early-season snowmaking and high base elevation (9,712 ft) make it one of the first resorts in Colorado to open competition-grade terrain park features each fall. US team athletes consistently use Copper for October–November pre-season training.
- **Grand Prix Qualifier** — Has served as a US Olympic qualifying competition venue for various disciplines.

## Terrain Park & Pipe Specs
- **Superpipe:** 22-foot walls, 550-foot length; built to Dew Tour specifications for December competition and maintained through spring
- **Dew Tour Slopestyle Course:** Built annually to competition specification; three jump features and six rail elements in standard configuration
- **Woodward Copper** — A year-round progression and training facility on resort; features foam pits, trampolines, and dry-slope training areas used by professional athletes for trick development outside of snow sessions

## Hosting History
Copper Mountain became the anchor Dew Tour venue when the event restructured and selected a permanent home. The December timing at Copper Mountain — early in the competitive season — gives it significant strategic value as a form guide for the rest of the winter. The resort's investment in Woodward Copper, a professional training facility integrated into the mountain, has further elevated its status as a year-round action sports training destination.
