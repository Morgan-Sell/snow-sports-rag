# Resort Profile: Vail Mountain

## Overview
- **Location:** Vail, Colorado
- **Summit Elevation:** 11,570 ft
- **Base Elevation:** 8,120 ft
- **Vertical Drop:** 3,450 ft
- **Annual Snowfall:** 354 inches
- **Skiable Acres:** 5,317 acres
- **State:** Colorado

Vail Mountain is one of the most iconic ski resorts in the world and the flagship property of Vail Resorts. It is most significant in the context of this knowledge base as the primary alpine ski training base for US alpine racer Mikaela Shiffrin, who grew up in Vail and trained at the mountain throughout her developmental years. Vail is also home to the Golden Peak race venue and the Birds of Prey downhill course at nearby Beaver Creek — the latter being one of the most storied speed ski race venues in the world. The resort is deeply embedded in the culture of American alpine ski racing.

## Competition Infrastructure
- **Birds of Prey (Beaver Creek)** — While technically at Beaver Creek Resort (adjacent to Vail and operated by Vail Resorts), the Birds of Prey downhill and super-G course is the most significant alpine race venue in the US. It has hosted the FIS Alpine World Cup Birds of Prey event for decades and hosted the 2015 FIS Alpine World Championships.
- **Golden Peak Race Venue (Vail)** — Vail Mountain's primary race hill; hosts domestic alpine race events and US team training.
- **US Ski & Snowboard Alpine Training** — Vail serves as a primary US alpine racing team training venue, particularly for technical disciplines (slalom, GS).

## Notable Athletes Who Train Here
- **Mikaela Shiffrin** — Grew up in Vail; primary home resort throughout her career; trained here from childhood through her ascent to the top of the FIS World Cup standings
- **Nina O'Brien** — Primary alpine training base; trains with US alpine team at Vail facilities
- **Ted Ligety** — Secondary training base; trained at Vail and Beaver Creek for speed discipline development (primary base was Park City)

## Terrain Description
Vail's most famous terrain feature is the Back Bowls — seven high-alpine, above-treeline bowls covering 3,000+ acres of open ski terrain. While not competition-infrastructure specific, the Back Bowls provide exceptional high-altitude all-mountain training terrain for alpine racers. The front side of the mountain contains the primary race venues and lift-accessed training areas used by the US alpine program.

## Hosting History
Beaver Creek — operated by Vail Resorts alongside Vail Mountain — has hosted the FIS Alpine World Cup Birds of Prey downhill event nearly every year since 1997, making it one of the longest-running World Cup venues in the US. The 2015 FIS Alpine World Ski Championships at Beaver Creek/Vail brought the sport's most prestigious non-Olympic event to the United States and produced several landmark performances by US athletes, including Ted Ligety's three gold medals at a single World Championships.
