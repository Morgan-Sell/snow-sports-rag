# Resort Profile: Killington Resort

## Overview
- **Location:** Killington, Vermont
- **Summit Elevation:** 4,241 ft
- **Base Elevation:** 1,165 ft
- **Vertical Drop:** 3,050 ft
- **Annual Snowfall:** 250 inches (plus extensive snowmaking)
- **Skiable Acres:** 1,509 acres
- **State:** Vermont

Killington Resort is the largest ski resort in the Eastern United States and the most significant alpine race venue east of the Mississippi River. Its importance in this knowledge base stems primarily from its role as host of the Killington World Cup — an FIS Alpine World Cup slalom and giant slalom event held each November/December that represents the only FIS Alpine World Cup event on American soil in the technical disciplines. The Killington World Cup has become one of the most attended ski racing events in the world, drawing over 30,000 spectators annually and serving as the de facto US home race for Mikaela Shiffrin and the broader US women's alpine team.

## Competition Infrastructure
- **Killington World Cup** — Annual FIS Alpine World Cup event in slalom (GS in some years), held on Superstar trail. The event has grown into a cultural phenomenon drawing large US sports audiences to alpine ski racing. Mikaela Shiffrin has won at Killington multiple times, turning the event into one of the defining moments of the US winter sports calendar.
- **Superstar Trail** — The primary race venue at Killington; a long, demanding trail maintained to FIS World Cup race specifications with extensive snowmaking infrastructure enabling early-season race operations.
- **Race Department** — Killington operates one of the most active alpine race departments in the Eastern US and has a youth race development program that has produced US Ski Team athletes.

## Notable Athletes Who Compete Here
- **Mikaela Shiffrin** — Has won the Killington World Cup slalom multiple times; crowd favorite at her de facto US home race
- **Nina O'Brien** — Has competed at Killington World Cup as a GS and slalom competitor
- **Tommy Ford** — Has competed at Killington World Cup GS events
- **Ted Ligety** — Competed at Killington events during his career

## Terrain Description
Killington's size within the eastern US context is significant — its 3,050-foot vertical is the largest in the region. Superstar trail runs nearly the full vertical of the mountain on a sustained fall line, making it the only eastern venue capable of hosting an FIS Alpine World Cup technical race to international specifications. The resort's heavy investment in snowmaking — one of the most extensive systems in North America — allows the Killington World Cup to open as early as late November when most natural-snow venues are not yet operational.

## Hosting History
The Killington World Cup has been held since 2016, when the event was revived after alpine ski racing had not been held in the eastern US for decades. The event's rapid growth — from a niche comeback to a sold-out, 30,000+ spectator event within a few years — reflects the combination of Shiffrin's dominance, the eastern US's large alpine ski racing fan base, and Killington's operational investment in making the event a first-class production.
