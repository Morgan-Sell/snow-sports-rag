# Resort Profile: Mammoth Mountain

## Overview
- **Location:** Mammoth Lakes, California
- **Summit Elevation:** 11,053 ft
- **Base Elevation:** 7,953 ft
- **Vertical Drop:** 3,100 ft
- **Annual Snowfall:** 400 inches
- **Skiable Acres:** 3,500 acres
- **State:** California

Mammoth Mountain is one of the premier ski and snowboard competition and training venues in the United States. Located in the Eastern Sierra Nevada range in California, it operates one of the longest ski seasons in North America — typically from November through June, and occasionally into August — due to its high base elevation and heavy average snowfall. The resort has long served as the primary California training base for US Ski & Snowboard halfpipe and slopestyle athletes and has hosted major events including the US Grand Prix and Mammoth Mountain Grand Prix across multiple decades. Mammoth's combination of a high-altitude superpipe, extensive terrain park, and season length make it uniquely valuable in the national athlete development system.

## Competition Infrastructure
Mammoth Mountain hosts and has historically hosted several major events:
- **US Grand Prix / Mammoth Grand Prix** — Has hosted USSS Olympic qualifier events at various points; the resort's halfpipe and slopestyle venue infrastructure meets FIS World Cup specifications.
- **Halfpipe Training Facility** — One of the premier dedicated superpipes in the US, used as the primary California-based team training venue by US Ski & Snowboard.
- **Mammoth Park & Pipe** — Extensive terrain park across multiple zones with dedicated competition course build capability.

## Terrain Park & Pipe Specs
- **Superpipe:** 22-foot walls, 550-foot length, FIS-specification construction; rated among the top three maintained halfpipes in the US
- **Main Park:** Extensive jump and rail features across multiple difficulty levels
- **Competition Slopestyle Course:** Configurable to international competition standards
- **Season Length:** November through June; one of the longest maintained pipe and park seasons in the country, enabling year-round training without requiring international travel

## Hosting History
Mammoth Mountain has hosted Grand Prix events at various points across the US competitive snowboard and freeski calendar. The resort's close association with Chloe Kim — who grew up as a Mammoth local and credits the resort's facilities for her early technical development — has brought significant media attention to its halfpipe program. Mammoth's extended season has made it especially valuable as a spring training venue when European and other North American resorts have closed.
