# Resort Profile: Aspen Snowmass

## Overview
- **Location:** Aspen, Colorado
- **Mountains:** Aspen Mountain, Aspen Highlands, Buttermilk Mountain, Snowmass
- **Summit Elevation (Snowmass):** 12,510 ft
- **Vertical Drop:** 4,406 ft (Snowmass, greatest in the US)
- **Annual Snowfall:** 300 inches
- **Skiable Acres:** 5,527 acres across four mountains
- **State:** Colorado

Aspen Snowmass is one of the most iconic ski and snowboard destinations in the world and the spiritual home of US action sports competition. The resort complex encompasses four mountains — Aspen Mountain, Aspen Highlands, Buttermilk Mountain, and Snowmass — with the competition-critical venue being Buttermilk Mountain, which serves as the permanent home of X Games Aspen. Buttermilk's Freeway terrain park and the adjacent superpipe are purpose-built and annually reconstructed to X Games specifications, making them the most watched competition features in snowboarding and freeskiing. Aspen Snowmass has a deep cultural connection to US action sports and hosts some of the sport's most historically significant events.

## Competition Infrastructure
- **X Games Aspen** — The flagship event held each January at Buttermilk Mountain. Buttermilk's Freeway run hosts the halfpipe, slopestyle, and big air competitions for both snowboard and freeski disciplines. The superpipe is built to 22-foot walls and 600-foot length for X Games; the slopestyle course includes three jump features and up to six rail elements. X Games Aspen has been held here continuously since 2002 and is the highest-profile domestic action sports competition in the world.
- **US Alpine Championships** — Aspen Mountain has hosted elite alpine ski race events including US Alpine Championship events.
- **World Cup Alpine** — Aspen has hosted FIS Alpine World Cup events including downhill and slalom on Aspen Mountain's historic course.

## Terrain Park & Pipe Specs (Buttermilk / X Games configuration)
- **X Games Superpipe:** 22-foot walls, 600-foot length, built to gold-standard specifications annually for X Games
- **X Games Slopestyle Course:** Three jump features capable of generating 70+ feet of amplitude; six rail features including both natural-style and urban rail elements
- **X Games Big Air Kicker:** Custom-built for the event; landing zone designed for maximum athlete safety at extreme amplitudes
- **General Terrain Parks:** Multiple parks across Buttermilk and Snowmass for resort guests

## Hosting History
Buttermilk Mountain has served as the X Games Aspen venue since 2002, making it the longest-running elite snowboard and freeski competition venue in US history. The combination of its Aspen setting, celebrity attendance, and ESPN broadcast reach has made X Games Aspen the single most visible domestic competition in snowboarding and freeskiing. The resort town of Aspen itself has a storied relationship with ski culture, from the FIS Alpine World Cup events on Aspen Mountain's Bell Mountain course to the historic Roch Cup, one of the oldest ski races in North America.
