# Resort Profile: Palisades Tahoe

## Overview
- **Location:** Olympic Valley, California
- **Summit Elevation:** 9,050 ft
- **Base Elevation:** 6,200 ft
- **Vertical Drop:** 2,850 ft
- **Annual Snowfall:** 450 inches
- **Skiable Acres:** 6,000 acres
- **State:** California
- **Formerly known as:** Squaw Valley USA (until 2021 renaming)

Palisades Tahoe is one of the most storied ski resorts in American history. It hosted the 1960 Winter Olympics — the first Olympics at which a significant television broadcast audience was present — and produced a generation of iconic US alpine ski racers. The resort has a direct lineage to American ski racing mythology and remains a top California training venue. Palisades Tahoe is the primary home resort of Jamie Anderson, who grew up in nearby South Lake Tahoe and has credited the Lake Tahoe ski community with shaping her snowboarding identity. The resort features expert terrain and a progressive terrain park operation.

## Competition Infrastructure
- **GS and Downhill Race Venue** — KT-22 and the Mountain Run provide race-specification alpine courses. The resort hosted the Chevrolet Tahoe FIS Alpine World Cup and World Cup events across its history.
- **Halfpipe and Terrain Park** — A maintained superpipe and terrain park at Shirley Lake and surrounding areas; used for athlete training and recreational riding.
- **1960 Winter Olympics Venue** — The resort's Olympic connection is deep; the 1960 Squaw Valley Winter Olympics were held here and the legacy infrastructure shaped the resort's competitive culture.

## Notable Athletes Who Train Here
- **Jamie Anderson** — Grew up in South Lake Tahoe; primary home resort; trained here throughout her career and identifies deeply with the Lake Tahoe ski and snowboard community
- **Brita Sigourney** — California-based halfpipe training camp visits
- **US Ski & Snowboard Alpine Team** — Pre-season alpine training camps

## Terrain Description
Palisades Tahoe is defined by high-consequence expert terrain, including KT-22 — one of the most challenging in-bounds alpine runs in the United States — and Granite Chief Wilderness terrain. The mountain's exposure and varied aspects provide natural training conditions for both alpine racers and freeriders. The Lake Tahoe region receives some of the heaviest average snowfall in North America, with moisture-laden Pacific storm systems creating deep, dense snowpack.

## Hosting History
Palisades Tahoe hosted the 1960 Winter Olympics, which included alpine skiing, Nordic events, and ski jumping. The resort hosted FIS Alpine World Cup events at multiple points across the decades and has maintained an active domestic competition calendar. The 2021 name change from Squaw Valley — a name considered offensive to Indigenous communities — to Palisades Tahoe was accompanied by significant community discussion and represents an important cultural moment in US ski resort history.
