# Resort Profile: Sun Valley

## Overview
- **Location:** Sun Valley, Idaho
- **Summit Elevation:** 9,150 ft (Bald Mountain)
- **Base Elevation:** 5,750 ft
- **Vertical Drop:** 3,400 ft
- **Annual Snowfall:** 220 inches
- **Skiable Acres:** 2,054 acres
- **State:** Idaho

Sun Valley Resort is America's first destination ski resort, opened in 1936 by Union Pacific Railroad chairman Averell Harriman. It is home to Bald Mountain — known as "Baldy" — which is widely regarded as one of the finest alpine ski mountains in North America, with sustained steep vertical and meticulously groomed runs that produce ideal conditions for alpine race training. Sun Valley hosts the US Alpine Ski Championships, the premier domestic alpine racing competition in the United States, and has served as a primary alpine racing development and competition venue for generations of US ski racers. Mikaela Shiffrin has been a regular competitor and champion at the US Alpine Championships held here.

## Competition Infrastructure
- **US Alpine Championships** — Sun Valley is the primary and most frequent host of the US Alpine Ski Championships, the national championship event across all alpine disciplines (slalom, giant slalom, super-G, downhill, alpine combined). Multiple championships across different years have been held on Baldy's race terrain.
- **Dollar Mountain** — The resort's second, smaller mountain used for beginner and intermediate skiing and terrain park features.
- **Alpine Race Courses** — Baldy's sustained vertical and consistent fall lines create ideal alpine race course terrain; several named courses are used for FIS-level domestic events.

## Notable Athletes Who Train or Have Competed Here
- **Mikaela Shiffrin** — Perennial US Alpine Championships competitor and champion; has won multiple US titles at Sun Valley
- **Ted Ligety** — Multiple US Alpine Championships victories at Sun Valley (now retired)
- **Tommy Ford** — US Alpine Championships competitor; races at Sun Valley annually
- **Nina O'Brien** — US Alpine Championships competitor
- **Ryan Cochran-Siegle** — US Alpine Championships speed event competitor

## Terrain Description
Bald Mountain is defined by long, sustained verticals with consistent fall lines that produce the ideal biomechanical conditions for alpine race training. Its 3,400-foot vertical is uninterrupted by flat sections — a rarity among North American resorts — making it particularly valued for downhill and super-G training. The consistent snowpack and meticulous grooming maintained by the resort have made Baldy a benchmark for alpine race course conditioning in North America.

## Hosting History
Sun Valley has hosted US Alpine Ski Championships across multiple decades and is the most historically significant domestic alpine race venue in the US. The resort's connection to early American ski culture — Ernest Hemingway famously spent time in Sun Valley and is buried nearby — gives it a cultural cachet that enhances its status as the premier US alpine championship venue.
