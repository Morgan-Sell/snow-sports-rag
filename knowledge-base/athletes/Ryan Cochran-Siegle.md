# Athlete Profile: Ryan Cochran-Siegle

## Summary
- **Discipline(s):** Super-G, Downhill, Giant Slalom
- **Hometown:** Starksboro, Vermont
- **Home Resort:** Sugarbush Resort, Vermont
- **Born:** January 27, 1992
- **Turned Pro:** 2011
- **Stance:** N/A (alpine ski racing)

## Career Highlights
Ryan Cochran-Siegle is the son of 1972 Olympic slalom champion Barbara Cochran, making him part of ski racing royalty. He is one of the best American speed skiers in the current era, with a World Cup super-G victory to his name and an Olympic silver medal in super-G at the 2022 Beijing Winter Olympics — one of the most unexpected and celebrated results in US alpine skiing history. Cochran-Siegle is a member of the famous Cochran family from Vermont, which has operated Cochran's Ski Area and produced multiple Olympic-level ski racers. His silver in Beijing — coming after multiple surgeries and a near-career-ending neck injury — was one of the most dramatic American Olympic performances in recent memory.

## Career Progression
- **2011–2015:** Developed on the FIS Alpine World Cup circuit in GS, super-G, and downhill. Consistent top-30 results but not yet breaking through to the podium.
- **2016–2017:** Began posting top-15 World Cup super-G results. Named to the US Ski Team A-squad. Competed at FIS Alpine World Championships.
- **2018:** Competed at the Pyeongchang Winter Olympics in super-G and GS. Finished outside the podium but posted competitive results. Sustained a knee injury following the Games.
- **2019:** Recovered from knee injury. Won his first FIS Alpine World Cup super-G event — a landmark moment for US men's speed skiing. Established himself as a genuine World Cup super-G threat.
- **2020:** Sustained a severe neck injury in a crash during a training run in Beaver Creek, Colorado. Required surgery and extensive rehabilitation. Many observers feared the injury might end his career.
- **2021:** Made a full return to World Cup competition — one of the most impressive rehabilitation stories in US skiing. Won multiple World Cup super-G top-10 finishes in his comeback season.
- **2022:** Won Olympic silver in super-G at the Beijing Winter Olympics — a result that stunned and delighted the US ski community. The silver came after one of the most challenging injury recoveries in recent US alpine history. Won additional World Cup super-G podiums in the same season.
- **2023:** Continued as a top World Cup super-G competitor. Won multiple podiums. Competed at FIS World Championships.

## Competition Circuit
Cochran-Siegle competes in super-G as primary, downhill and GS as secondary:
- **FIS Alpine World Cup** — super-G specialist; World Cup event winner
- **Winter Olympic Games** — silver medal (2022 Beijing, super-G)
- **FIS Alpine World Championships** — US team member, podium contender
- **US Alpine Championships** (Sun Valley, Idaho) — competitor
- **Killington World Cup** (Killington, Vermont) — US home event appearance

## Sponsorships

## Performance Notes
Cochran-Siegle's speed skiing is characterized by exceptional commitment through high-speed sections and aggressive line choices in super-G. His Vermont background — unusual among elite speed skiers, who typically grow up at high-altitude Western US or European resorts — is credited by coaches for developing a technical precision that complements his natural fearlessness in downhill and super-G. He trains with the US Ski & Snowboard alpine speed team and spends significant time at European speed training venues during the winter race calendar. His family's Cochran's Ski Area in Vermont remains a touchstone for the Vermont ski community and a symbol of American grassroots ski racing culture.
