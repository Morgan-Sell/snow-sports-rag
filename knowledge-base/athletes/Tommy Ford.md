# Athlete Profile: Tommy Ford

## Summary
- **Discipline(s):** Giant Slalom, Super-G
- **Hometown:** Bend, Oregon
- **Home Resort:** Mount Bachelor, Oregon
- **Born:** March 17, 1989
- **Turned Pro:** 2008
- **Stance:** N/A (alpine ski racing)

## Career Highlights
Tommy Ford is the most accomplished active American men's giant slalom ski racer and the spiritual successor to Ted Ligety in the US alpine GS program. He has won multiple FIS Alpine World Cup giant slalom events, becoming one of only a handful of US men to achieve regular GS podiums in the modern era of the sport. Ford is known for his technically precise racing line and exceptional edge performance — attributes he developed training at Mount Bachelor in Oregon and later at Park City Mountain Resort with the US Ski & Snowboard alpine racing team. He has competed at multiple Winter Olympics and World Championships.

## Career Progression
- **2008–2012:** Competed on the FIS Alpine World Cup circuit in GS and super-G. Consistent top-20 finishes established him as a legitimate World Cup racer but not yet a podium contender.
- **2013–2015:** Improved to consistent top-10 finishes in GS. Named to US Olympic Team for Sochi. Competed at FIS Alpine World Championships in Beaver Creek, Colorado.
- **2016–2018:** Broke through with first World Cup GS podiums. Competed at Pyeongchang Winter Olympics. Began training alongside Ted Ligety at Park City, absorbing technical coaching on edge mechanics.
- **2019:** Won first FIS Alpine World Cup giant slalom event — a landmark achievement for US men's alpine. Podiumed at multiple GS World Cup events in the same season.
- **2020–2021:** Continued World Cup GS podiums. Won super-G events as a secondary discipline. Named to the US Olympic Team for Beijing.
- **2022:** Competed at the Beijing Winter Olympics in GS. Won Killington World Cup GS podium in front of a home crowd — one of his most emotionally resonant results.
- **2023:** Won multiple FIS World Cup GS events, cementing his status as the best US men's GS racer in the post-Ligety era. Competed at FIS Alpine World Championships.

## Competition Circuit
Ford competes in GS as primary, super-G as secondary:
- **FIS Alpine World Cup** — GS specialist; multiple wins and podiums
- **Winter Olympic Games** — multiple appearances
- **FIS Alpine World Championships** — US team member; podium contender
- **US Alpine Championships** (Sun Valley, Idaho) — domestic champion
- **Killington World Cup** (Killington, Vermont) — US home event; strong results

## Sponsorships
Head (primary skis and boots), Red Bull, Oakley, Atomic (early career)

## Performance Notes
Ford's GS technique combines exceptional hip angulation — developed in part through exposure to Ted Ligety's training methods at Park City — with a calm, controlled racing demeanor that allows him to execute technical sections without overcooking entries. He grew up racing at Mount Bachelor in Oregon, which is credited with developing the well-rounded skiing foundation that enabled his later technical refinement. He now trains primarily at Park City Mountain Resort with the US Ski & Snowboard alpine program and views the Killington World Cup as a personal highlight of each season given the rare opportunity to race in front of a US home crowd.
