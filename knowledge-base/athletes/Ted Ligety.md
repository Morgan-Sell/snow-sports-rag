# Athlete Profile: Ted Ligety

## Summary
- **Discipline(s):** Giant Slalom, Super-G, Alpine Combined (retired)
- **Hometown:** Salt Lake City, Utah
- **Home Resort:** Park City Mountain Resort, Utah
- **Born:** August 31, 1984
- **Turned Pro:** 2002
- **Status:** Retired (2019)

## Career Highlights
Ted Ligety is one of the greatest giant slalom ski racers in history. He is a two-time Olympic gold medalist (alpine combined at Turin 2006 and giant slalom at Sochi 2014) and a five-time FIS Alpine World Championships gold medalist — the most World Championship gold medals of any American alpine skier. Ligety is universally credited with revolutionizing giant slalom technique: his extreme hip angulation and carving arc — sometimes called "the Ligety turn" — redefined the technical and biomechanical limits of the discipline and is now taught in ski racing academies worldwide. He retired following the 2018–2019 season.

## Career Progression
- **2002–2005:** Competed on the FIS Alpine World Cup circuit in GS and super-G. Early results were solid but not yet dominant.
- **2006:** Won Olympic gold in the alpine combined at the Turin Winter Olympics — a surprise victory that announced him as a world-class talent.
- **2008–2010:** Developed his revolutionary GS carving technique. Won multiple FIS World Cup GS events and began to establish statistical dominance in the discipline.
- **2011:** Won FIS Alpine World Championships gold in giant slalom in Garmisch-Partenkirchen, Germany.
- **2012–2013:** Dominated the World Cup GS circuit with an unprecedented run of consecutive wins. Won the FIS World Cup GS overall title. Coached in part by the Ligety-specific technical team at Park City.
- **2013:** Won FIS Alpine World Championships gold in giant slalom in Schladming — his second World Championship GS title.
- **2014:** Won Olympic gold in giant slalom at the Sochi Winter Olympics, cementing his status as the greatest GS racer of his era. His winning margin was among the largest in Olympic GS history.
- **2015:** Won FIS Alpine World Championships gold in giant slalom, super-G, and combined in Beaver Creek, Colorado — three golds at one event on his home country's soil.
- **2016–2018:** Managed knee and back injuries. Won additional World Cup events but with reduced frequency. Competed at the Pyeongchang Winter Olympics, finishing outside the podium due to injury.
- **2019:** Announced retirement from professional ski racing. Continued involvement in ski development and youth programs.

## Competition Circuit
During his career, Ligety competed across GS, super-G, and combined:
- **FIS Alpine World Cup** — GS specialist; all-time dominant force
- **Winter Olympic Games** — two-time gold medalist (2006 Turin combined, 2014 Sochi GS)
- **FIS Alpine World Championships** — five gold medals; most by any US alpine skier
- **US Alpine Championships** (Sun Valley, Idaho) — domestic champion multiple times
- **Killington World Cup** (Killington, Vermont) — competitor

## Sponsorships
Head (primary skis and boots), Red Bull, Atomic (early career), Smith Optics

## Performance Notes
Ligety's "extreme carving" technique involved an unprecedented degree of inside-hip drop and edge engagement that allowed him to carry far more speed through GS gates than any competitor could match. Biomechanics researchers have published academic analyses of his turning mechanics. He trained based out of Park City Mountain Resort in Utah, home of the US Ski & Snowboard alpine racing program, and served as a mentor to the next generation of US GS racers throughout the latter portion of his career. His Park City training partnerships with Tommy Ford and other US GS athletes helped accelerate the development of American giant slalom across his competitive lifetime.
