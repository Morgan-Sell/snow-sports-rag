# Athlete Profile: Torin Yater-Wallace

## Summary
- **Discipline(s):** Halfpipe Freeski
- **Hometown:** Aspen, Colorado
- **Home Resort:** Aspen Snowmass, Colorado
- **Born:** October 28, 1994
- **Turned Pro:** 2011
- **Stance:** Regular

## Career Highlights
Torin Yater-Wallace is a two-time Olympian and one of the most technically proficient men's halfpipe freeskiers in US history. He first competed at X Games Aspen at age 14 and won his first X Games halfpipe medal at 16. As an Aspen native who literally grew up at the competition venue — Buttermilk Mountain — Yater-Wallace has an extraordinary connection to the X Games environment. He is a multiple X Games Aspen halfpipe medalist and FIS Freeski World Cup podium finisher. His skating-influenced style and creative trick selection have made him one of the most watchable pipe skiers of his generation.

## Career Progression
- **2009–2011:** Began competing professionally at a remarkably young age. First X Games Aspen appearance at 14. Won first X Games Aspen halfpipe medal at 16 — becoming one of the youngest X Games halfpipe medalists in history.
- **2012–2013:** Multiple X Games Aspen halfpipe medals. Won FIS Freeski World Cup halfpipe events. Named to the US Olympic long team. Battled a serious knee injury that required surgery and rehabilitation.
- **2014:** Competed at the Sochi Winter Olympics in halfpipe. Won X Games Aspen halfpipe silver in the same season.
- **2015–2017:** Won multiple X Games Aspen halfpipe medals. Won FIS Freeski World Cup halfpipe events. Won Dew Tour halfpipe at Copper Mountain.
- **2018:** Competed at the Pyeongchang Winter Olympics in halfpipe. Won X Games Aspen halfpipe gold — his first X Games gold medal at his home mountain. A landmark moment for an Aspen native.
- **2019–2021:** Continued competing at the top World Cup level. Won multiple X Games Aspen halfpipe medals. Competed at Dew Tour halfpipe at Copper Mountain.
- **2022–2023:** Remained competitive on the X Games and FIS circuits. One of the most experienced members of the US halfpipe freeski team and a mentor for younger athletes.

## Competition Circuit
Yater-Wallace competes exclusively in halfpipe freeski:
- **X Games Aspen** (Buttermilk Mountain / Aspen Snowmass) — his home mountain; multiple gold and silver medals
- **Winter Olympic Games** — two-time Olympian (2014 Sochi, 2018 Pyeongchang)
- **FIS Freeski World Cup** — halfpipe circuit, multiple wins
- **Dew Tour** (Copper Mountain) — multiple halfpipe medals
- **US Grand Prix** (Park City Mountain Resort, Mammoth Mountain) — regular competitor

## Performance Notes
Yater-Wallace brings a skating-influenced aesthetic to halfpipe freeskiing — his runs flow with a creative, organic quality that distinguishes him from athletes who approach the pipe in a more mechanical, trick-by-trick manner. He executes double cork 1260s and 1440s with amplitude that frequently exceeds 20 feet above the pipe lip. Growing up skiing at Aspen Snowmass and training specifically at Buttermilk Mountain — the X Games venue — has given him a competitive advantage at that event that few rivals can match. He works with the US Ski & Snowboard halfpipe coaching staff and also coaches younger athletes in the Aspen development pipeline.
