# Athlete Profile: Nina O'Brien

## Summary
- **Discipline(s):** Giant Slalom, Slalom, Super-G
- **Hometown:** San Francisco, California
- **Home Resort:** Vail Mountain, Colorado
- **Born:** October 8, 1997
- **Turned Pro:** 2017
- **Stance:** N/A (alpine ski racing)

## Career Highlights
Nina O'Brien is one of the most promising and resilient young alpine ski racers in the United States. She won a silver medal in giant slalom at the 2021 FIS Alpine World Championships in Cortina d'Ampezzo, Italy — a breakthrough result that positioned her as a future World Cup contender in the technical disciplines. O'Brien is known for her fearless, aggressive racing style and exceptional GS carving ability. Her career has been interrupted by serious injuries — most notably a severe leg fracture during the 2022 Beijing Winter Olympics — but she has returned to elite competition each time with characteristic determination.

## Career Progression
- **2017:** Turned professional. Began competing on the FIS Alpine World Cup circuit in GS and slalom.
- **2018–2019:** Consistent top-20 World Cup finishes in GS. Developed technical profile as a pure technical racer with growing speed discipline capability.
- **2020–2021:** Won silver medal in giant slalom at the FIS Alpine World Championships in Cortina d'Ampezzo, Italy — the most significant result of her career to that point. Won multiple World Cup top-10 finishes in GS.
- **2022 January:** Competed at the Beijing Winter Olympics in giant slalom. Sustained a severe compound fracture to her lower left leg during the second run of the GS event — one of the most difficult injuries witnessed at a Winter Olympics in recent years. Was airlifted and required emergency surgery.
- **2022 Recovery:** Underwent multiple surgeries and extensive rehabilitation. Documented her recovery publicly and became an inspiration for resilience within the US ski community.
- **2023:** Returned to FIS Alpine World Cup competition. Posted multiple top-20 GS results in her comeback season. Won a World Cup GS top-10 finish — a remarkable return. Nominated for US Ski & Snowboard Comeback Athlete recognition.

## Competition Circuit
O'Brien competes in GS as primary, with slalom and super-G:
- **FIS Alpine World Cup** — GS specialist; podium and top-10 results
- **Winter Olympic Games** — competed 2022 Beijing (GS)
- **FIS Alpine World Championships** — silver medal (2021 Cortina, GS)
- **US Alpine Championships** (Sun Valley, Idaho) — US team competitor
- **Killington World Cup** (Killington, Vermont) — US home event appearance

## Sponsorships
Rossignol (primary skis and boots), Red Bull, Oakley

## Performance Notes
O'Brien's GS racing combines aggressive arc initiation with high-line entries that allow her to carry exceptional speed through the course's flat sections. Her athletic background includes gymnastics training in her youth, which coaches credit for the body awareness and balance that underlies her technical carving. She trains based out of Vail Mountain with the US Ski & Snowboard alpine racing team and has become a central figure in the next generation of US women's alpine after the injury and recovery arc that has defined the 2022–2023 period of her career.
