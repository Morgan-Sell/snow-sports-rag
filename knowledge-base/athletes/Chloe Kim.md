# Athlete Profile: Chloe Kim

## Summary
- **Discipline(s):** Halfpipe Snowboard, Slopestyle Snowboard
- **Hometown:** Long Beach, California
- **Home Resort:** Mammoth Mountain, California
- **Born:** April 23, 2000
- **Turned Pro:** 2015
- **Stance:** Regular

## Career Highlights
Chloe Kim is widely regarded as the greatest women's halfpipe snowboarder in history. She became the youngest woman to win an Olympic snowboard gold medal at the 2018 Winter Olympics in Pyeongchang at age 17, posting a near-perfect run with back-to-back 1080s. She defended that title at the 2022 Winter Olympics in Beijing, becoming the first woman to win back-to-back Olympic halfpipe gold medals. Her X Games record is equally historic — she has won multiple gold medals at X Games Aspen across halfpipe and slopestyle, first competing at age 13. Kim has been a perennial FIS Snowboard World Cup halfpipe champion, regularly posting scores above 90 in competition.

## Career Progression
- **2013:** First appeared at X Games Aspen at age 13, finishing 4th in slopestyle — the youngest competitor in the field.
- **2015:** Turned professional. Won three gold medals at X Games Aspen, becoming the first athlete to accomplish this in a single year. Earned FIS World Cup halfpipe title.
- **2016:** Won US Grand Prix halfpipe title at Mammoth Mountain. Named to US Olympic long team.
- **2017:** Won FIS Snowboard World Championships halfpipe gold in Sierra Nevada, Spain. Became the first woman to land back-to-back 1080s in competition at the Burton US Open at Stratton Mountain.
- **2018:** Won Olympic gold at the Pyeongchang Winter Olympics (halfpipe). Named AP Female Athlete of the Year.
- **2019:** Took a gap year from competition to attend Princeton University, focusing on academics and mental health.
- **2020–2021:** Returned to competition. Swept the FIS World Cup halfpipe circuit, winning four of five events. Won X Games Aspen halfpipe gold.
- **2022:** Won Olympic gold at the Beijing Winter Olympics (halfpipe), posting a score of 94.00 in the final. Won Burton US Open halfpipe at Stratton Mountain.
- **2023:** Won X Games Aspen halfpipe gold. Captured FIS Snowboard World Cup overall halfpipe title. Named US Ski & Snowboard Athlete of the Year.

## Competition Circuit
Kim competes primarily in the halfpipe discipline across the following events:
- **X Games Aspen** (Buttermilk Mountain / Aspen Snowmass) — perennial gold medalist
- **Winter Olympic Games** — two-time champion (2018, 2022)
- **FIS Snowboard World Cup** — halfpipe circuit, multiple season titles
- **Burton US Open Snowboarding Championships** (Stratton Mountain, VT) — multiple wins
- **US Grand Prix** (Park City Mountain Resort) — multiple wins
- **Dew Tour** (Copper Mountain) — occasional halfpipe appearance

## Sponsorships
Burton Snowboards (primary), Monster Energy, Toyota, Panda Express, Oakley, Visa

## Performance Notes
Kim is known for her technical amplitude — consistently reaching 20+ feet above the pipe lip — and her ability to perform consecutive 1080s with control and style. Her competitive scores rarely fall below 88.00. She trains annually at Mammoth Mountain and completes pre-season halfpipe camps at Copper Mountain. Her coaching team has been anchored by longtime US Ski & Snowboard halfpipe coach Ricky Bower.
