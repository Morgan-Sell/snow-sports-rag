# Athlete Profile: Nick Goepper

## Summary
- **Discipline(s):** Slopestyle Freeski, Big Air Freeski
- **Hometown:** Lawrenceburg, Indiana
- **Home Resort:** Steamboat Springs, Colorado
- **Born:** November 15, 1993
- **Turned Pro:** 2011
- **Stance:** Regular

## Career Highlights
Nick Goepper is the most decorated American men's slopestyle freeskier in Olympic history. He is a three-time Olympic medalist — silver at the 2014 Sochi Winter Olympics and silver and bronze at the 2022 Beijing Winter Olympics — making him one of only a handful of US freeskiers to podium across three consecutive Olympic cycles. Goepper is a multiple X Games Aspen slopestyle gold medalist and one of the defining figures of the slopestyle freeski discipline since its Olympic debut. His journey has included overcoming severe depression and substance abuse issues, which he has shared publicly, making him an important mental health advocate within the action sports community.

## Career Progression
- **2011:** Turned professional at age 17. Began competing on the FIS Freeski World Cup slopestyle circuit.
- **2012–2013:** Became one of the top men's slopestyle freeskiers in the world. Won multiple X Games Aspen slopestyle gold medals. Earned FIS World Cup slopestyle titles.
- **2014:** Won Olympic silver in slopestyle freeski at the Sochi Winter Olympics — the discipline's Olympic debut. One of the most prominent breakout moments in US action sports history.
- **2015–2016:** Stepped back from competition to address personal struggles with alcohol and mental health. Was open publicly about these challenges, earning widespread respect in the sports community.
- **2017–2018:** Made a successful comeback to elite competition. Won X Games Aspen slopestyle bronze. Competed at Pyeongchang Winter Olympics, finishing in the top 10 but outside the podium.
- **2019–2021:** Won multiple FIS Freeski World Cup podiums. Won X Games Aspen slopestyle silver. Dominated Dew Tour slopestyle at Copper Mountain.
- **2022:** Won Olympic silver and bronze at the Beijing Winter Olympics in freeski slopestyle — an extraordinary redemption arc. Named US Olympic Committee Comeback Athlete of the Year.
- **2023:** Won X Games Aspen slopestyle silver. Competed on full FIS World Cup circuit. Remained one of the most technically consistent US freeskiers.

## Competition Circuit
Goepper competes primarily in slopestyle with big air as a secondary:
- **X Games Aspen** (Buttermilk Mountain / Aspen Snowmass) — multiple slopestyle gold medals
- **Winter Olympic Games** — three-time medalist (2014, 2022)
- **FIS Freeski World Cup** — slopestyle circuit, multiple season titles
- **Dew Tour** (Copper Mountain) — dominant competitor
- **US Grand Prix** (Park City Mountain Resort) — multiple wins

## Sponsorships
Atomic Skis (primary), Red Bull, Oakley, Electric, J.Lindeberg

## Performance Notes
Goepper's slopestyle skiing is defined by massive amplitude on jump features and creative rail combinations. He is known for his switch 1440 and double cork combinations, which he executes with exceptional consistency under pressure. He trains at Steamboat Springs, Colorado, which has maintained a progressive terrain park for the US Ski & Snowboard freeski slopestyle program. Goepper's mental health advocacy work, including his documentary film, has made him a highly visible ambassador for the sport beyond competitive results.
