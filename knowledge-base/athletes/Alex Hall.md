# Athlete Profile: Alex Hall

## Summary
- **Discipline(s):** Slopestyle Freeski, Big Air Freeski
- **Hometown:** Park City, Utah
- **Home Resort:** Park City Mountain Resort, Utah
- **Born:** January 9, 1998
- **Turned Pro:** 2018
- **Stance:** Regular

## Career Highlights
Alex Hall is the reigning king of men's slopestyle freeskiing, widely regarded as the most stylistically gifted big mountain and park skier in the world. He won the FIS Freeski World Championships slopestyle gold in 2021 and 2023 and is the current FIS Freeski World Cup slopestyle overall champion. Hall is known for blending technical difficulty with an almost absurd level of casual style — his runs look effortless in a sport where most athletes look like they are fighting for their lives. He is a multiple X Games Aspen medalist and a growing force in big air. Born and raised in Park City, he is a product of the US Ski & Snowboard high-performance pipeline.

## Career Progression
- **2016–2018:** Competed on the junior FIS Freeski circuit with immediate results. Made his senior World Cup debut in 2017, earning podiums in his first season.
- **2018:** Turned professional. Won first FIS Freeski World Cup slopestyle event. Named to the US Olympic long team.
- **2019:** Won multiple FIS Freeski World Cup slopestyle events. Won X Games Aspen slopestyle silver. Named US Ski & Snowboard Freeski Athlete of the Year.
- **2020–2021:** Won FIS Freeski World Championships slopestyle gold in Aspen, Colorado. Won FIS Freeski World Cup slopestyle overall title. Won X Games Aspen slopestyle gold.
- **2022:** Competed at the Beijing Winter Olympics, finishing 4th in slopestyle — just off the podium in a heartbreaking result that underscored his medal-contention status. Won Dew Tour slopestyle gold at Copper Mountain.
- **2023:** Won FIS Freeski World Championships slopestyle gold (second world title). Won X Games Aspen slopestyle gold. Dominant across the full FIS World Cup slopestyle circuit.

## Competition Circuit
Hall competes primarily in slopestyle with big air as a secondary:
- **X Games Aspen** (Buttermilk Mountain / Aspen Snowmass) — multiple gold medals
- **Winter Olympic Games** — competed 2022 Beijing
- **FIS Freeski World Cup** — slopestyle circuit, multiple season titles and world championship wins
- **Dew Tour** (Copper Mountain) — dominant competitor
- **US Grand Prix** (Park City Mountain Resort) — home event, multiple wins

## Sponsorships

## Performance Notes
Hall's defining characteristic is what coaches and commentators call "skier's style" — an ability to make technically demanding tricks look completely relaxed. His switch double cork 1260 and switch misty 1440 are among the most aesthetically praised tricks in competitive freeskiing. Growing up in Park City and training at Park City Mountain Resort has given him direct access to the US Ski & Snowboard high-performance center, which operates year-round park and pipe programs. Hall is frequently cited by peers and coaches as the athlete most likely to define the freeski slopestyle aesthetic for the next decade.
