# Athlete Profile: Maggie Voisin

## Summary
- **Discipline(s):** Slopestyle Freeski, Big Air Freeski
- **Hometown:** Whitefish, Montana
- **Home Resort:** Whitefish Mountain Resort, Montana
- **Born:** February 12, 1999
- **Turned Pro:** 2014
- **Stance:** Regular

## Career Highlights
Maggie Voisin is one of the most technically accomplished women's slopestyle freeskiers in the world. She qualified for the 2014 Sochi Winter Olympics at age 14 — making her one of the youngest US Winter Olympians in modern history — before sustaining an injury that prevented her from competing. She went on to compete at the 2018 Pyeongchang and 2022 Beijing Winter Olympics. Voisin is a multiple FIS Freeski World Cup slopestyle podium finisher and X Games Aspen medalist, known for blending progressive technical content with exceptional rail skill developed during her formative years at Whitefish Mountain Resort in her home state of Montana.

## Career Progression
- **2013–2014:** Emerged as a teenage prodigy on the junior and senior FIS circuits. Qualified for the 2014 Sochi Winter Olympics at age 14 — one of the youngest US Olympic qualifiers in any sport. Sustained a severe ankle injury during an Olympic training session in January 2014, preventing competition.
- **2015–2016:** Recovered from injury and returned to elite-level competition. Won multiple FIS Freeski World Cup podiums. Won X Games Aspen slopestyle bronze.
- **2017–2018:** Named to US Olympic Team for Pyeongchang. Competed at the 2018 Pyeongchang Winter Olympics in slopestyle. Won multiple World Cup podiums and X Games medals.
- **2019:** Won FIS Freeski World Cup slopestyle event. Won Dew Tour slopestyle gold at Copper Mountain.
- **2020–2021:** Won multiple FIS World Cup slopestyle and big air podiums. Won X Games Aspen slopestyle silver.
- **2022:** Competed at the Beijing Winter Olympics in slopestyle, reaching the final. Won FIS Freeski World Cup big air silver. Won Dew Tour slopestyle gold at Copper Mountain.
- **2023:** Won X Games Aspen slopestyle gold. Named to the US Ski & Snowboard team for the 2025–2026 Olympic cycle with gold medal projection.

## Competition Circuit
Voisin competes in slopestyle and big air:
- **X Games Aspen** (Buttermilk Mountain / Aspen Snowmass) — multiple slopestyle medals
- **Winter Olympic Games** — three-time qualifier/competitor (2014, 2018, 2022)
- **FIS Freeski World Cup** — slopestyle and big air circuits, multiple wins
- **Dew Tour** (Copper Mountain) — multiple gold medals
- **US Grand Prix** (Park City Mountain Resort) — regular competitor

## Sponsorships
Salomon (primary skis and boots), Red Bull, Oakley, Volcom

## Performance Notes
Voisin is distinguished by rail technique that rivals the best male slopestyle athletes in the world — her precision on bonks, presses, and technical grinds consistently earns the highest section scores among women. She grew up skiing at Whitefish Mountain Resort in Montana, an unusual training base compared to most elite US athletes, which she credits for developing well-rounded all-mountain skiing abilities that translate directly to creative slopestyle run construction. She completes annual training camps at Park City Mountain Resort with the US Ski & Snowboard slopestyle program.
