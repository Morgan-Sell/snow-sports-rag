# Athlete Profile: Shaun White

## Summary
- **Discipline(s):** Halfpipe Snowboard, Slopestyle Snowboard, Halfpipe Skateboard
- **Hometown:** San Diego, California
- **Home Resort:** Park City Mountain Resort, Utah
- **Born:** September 3, 1986
- **Turned Pro:** 2000
- **Stance:** Regular

## Career Highlights
Shaun White is the most decorated action sports athlete in US history. He is a three-time Olympic halfpipe snowboard gold medalist (2006, 2010, 2018), the only snowboarder to win three Olympic gold medals. White dominated X Games for two decades, winning a record 13 X Games gold medals in halfpipe snowboard alone, plus additional golds in skateboard halfpipe — the only athlete to win X Games gold in two different sports. He has won more than 80 World Cup events and holds the distinction of being the first snowboarder to land a Double McTwist 1260 in competition, a trick he debuted at the 2010 Olympics. White retired from competition following the 2022 Beijing Winter Olympics.

## Career Progression
- **2000:** Turned professional at age 13. Named youngest member of the US Snowboard Team.
- **2003:** Won his first X Games gold medal in halfpipe at X Games Aspen. Established himself as the sport's top emerging talent.
- **2006:** Won Olympic gold at Turin Winter Olympics (halfpipe), his first of three Olympic titles. Became a household name.
- **2008–2011:** Dominated the halfpipe circuit. Won consecutive X Games Aspen gold medals and multiple FIS World Cup titles. In 2010 won Olympic gold at Vancouver with a now-legendary run featuring the Double McTwist 1260.
- **2012–2013:** Battled injuries including shoulder and wrist surgeries but remained competitive. Won Dew Tour halfpipe events at Breckenridge.
- **2014:** Missed the podium at the 2014 Sochi Winter Olympics, finishing fourth — one of the rare setbacks of his career. Withdrew from slopestyle to focus on halfpipe.
- **2015–2017:** Returned to dominant form. Won multiple X Games Aspen halfpipe titles. Constructed a private halfpipe in New Zealand for solo Olympic preparation.
- **2018:** Won Olympic gold at Pyeongchang Winter Olympics (halfpipe) with a score of 97.75 on his final run, landing the first back-to-back Double Cork 1440s in Olympic competition. Became the first snowboarder to win three Olympic gold medals.
- **2019–2021:** Continued competing on the FIS World Cup and X Games circuits while managing health issues including open-heart surgery in 2019. Returned to competition with characteristic determination.
- **2022:** Competed in his final Olympics at the Beijing Winter Olympics, finishing fourth in the halfpipe final. Emotionally retired from competitive snowboarding on the final day of X Games Aspen 2022.

## Competition Circuit
White competed across the full calendar of premier events throughout his career:
- **X Games Aspen** (Buttermilk Mountain / Aspen Snowmass) — 13 halfpipe gold medals, most in X Games history
- **Winter Olympic Games** — three-time champion (2006, 2010, 2018)
- **FIS Snowboard World Cup** — halfpipe circuit, multiple season titles
- **US Grand Prix** (Park City Mountain Resort) — multiple wins
- **Dew Tour** (Breckenridge, Copper Mountain) — multiple halfpipe wins
- **Burton US Open Snowboarding Championships** (Stratton Mountain, VT) — multiple wins

## Sponsorships
Burton Snowboards (lifetime partnership), Red Bull, Oakley, BurtonUS, Target, Hewlett-Packard, and numerous others across his peak earning years. White was one of the highest-paid action sports athletes in the world during the 2006–2018 period.

## Performance Notes
White's signature technical elements included the Double McTwist 1260, the Double Cork 1440, and back-to-back 1080s performed with exceptional amplitude. His competitive scores frequently exceeded 94.00, and his 2010 Olympic qualification run — often called "The Tomahawk" — is considered the greatest halfpipe run in history. White trained primarily at Park City Mountain Resort and conducted private international camps at custom-built halfpipe facilities. He worked with coaches including JJ Thomas and the broader US Ski & Snowboard coaching staff throughout his career.
