# Athlete Profile: Mikaela Shiffrin

## Summary
- **Discipline(s):** Slalom, Giant Slalom, Super-G, Downhill, Alpine Combined
- **Hometown:** Vail, Colorado
- **Home Resort:** Vail Mountain, Colorado
- **Born:** March 13, 1995
- **Turned Pro:** 2011
- **Stance:** N/A (alpine ski racing)

## Career Highlights
Mikaela Shiffrin is the greatest alpine ski racer in history. She holds the all-time record for FIS Alpine World Cup wins — surpassing Ingemar Stenmark's long-standing record — with over 90 World Cup victories across all disciplines. She is a three-time Olympic medalist (gold in slalom at Sochi 2014, gold in giant slalom at Pyeongchang 2018, and bronze in super-G at Beijing 2022) and six-time overall FIS Alpine World Cup champion. Shiffrin has won the FIS Alpine World Championships multiple times and is the only skier in history to win World Cup races in six different disciplines. She is universally regarded as the GOAT of alpine ski racing.

## Career Progression
- **2011:** Turned professional at age 16. Named the top-ranked women's slalom skier in the US within her first competitive season.
- **2012:** Won her first FIS Alpine World Cup race in slalom at age 17, becoming one of the youngest World Cup winners in history.
- **2013:** Won the FIS Alpine World Championships gold in slalom in Schladming, Austria. Won first overall World Cup season title.
- **2014:** Won Olympic gold in slalom at the Sochi Winter Olympics at age 18 — the youngest Olympic slalom champion in history. Won second overall World Cup title.
- **2015–2016:** Continued dominant World Cup results, adding giant slalom and super-G to her slalom supremacy. Won FIS World Championships silver in super-G and bronze in combined.
- **2017:** Won FIS Alpine World Championships gold in slalom and gold in alpine combined in St. Moritz. Won third overall World Cup title.
- **2018:** Won Olympic gold in giant slalom at Pyeongchang Winter Olympics. Also competed in downhill, super-G, slalom, and combined — competing in all five alpine disciplines at one Games. Won fourth overall World Cup title.
- **2019:** Won FIS Alpine World Championships gold in slalom, giant slalom, and alpine combined in Are, Sweden — three gold medals at one Championships. Won fifth overall World Cup title.
- **2020:** Won sixth overall FIS World Cup title.
- **2021–2022:** Competed at the Beijing Winter Olympics, winning bronze in super-G. Dealt with grief after the passing of her father. Won FIS World Cup events in multiple disciplines, approaching Stenmark's all-time wins record.
- **2023:** Broke Ingemar Stenmark's all-time World Cup wins record. Won FIS Alpine World Championships gold in slalom in Courchevel, France. Continued as the dominant force in all alpine disciplines. Trains based out of Vail Mountain in Colorado.

## Competition Circuit
Shiffrin competes across all five FIS Alpine disciplines:
- **FIS Alpine World Cup** — all disciplines; all-time wins record holder
- **Winter Olympic Games** — three-time medalist (2014 Sochi, 2018 Pyeongchang, 2022 Beijing)
- **FIS Alpine World Championships** — multiple gold medals; most decorated US alpine skier in championship history
- **US Alpine Championships** (Sun Valley, Idaho) — domestic champion
- **Killington World Cup** (Killington, Vermont) — home-country World Cup event; perennial winner

## Performance Notes
Shiffrin's technical foundation in slalom is considered the finest in the history of the discipline — her edge-to-edge quickness and gate exit angles produce course times that are categorically different from the field. Her expansion into speed disciplines (super-G and downhill) is a testament to physical courage and technical adaptability. She trains based out of Vail Mountain in Colorado, where she grew up, and maintains a rigorous off-season conditioning program with a dedicated personal coaching team. Shiffrin is the central figure in the Killington World Cup — an event held in the United States that draws record crowds in large part due to her participation and connection to American ski culture.
