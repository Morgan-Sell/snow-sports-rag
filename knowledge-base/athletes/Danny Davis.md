# Athlete Profile: Danny Davis

## Summary
- **Discipline(s):** Halfpipe Snowboard
- **Hometown:** Rochester, Michigan
- **Home Resort:** Mammoth Mountain, California
- **Born:** August 3, 1988
- **Turned Pro:** 2005
- **Stance:** Regular

## Career Highlights
Danny Davis is one of the most creative and stylistically influential halfpipe snowboarders of his generation. While his Olympic record is defined by a bronze at the 2014 Sochi Winter Olympics, his impact on the sport extends far beyond podium finishes. Davis is a multiple X Games halfpipe medalist and a longtime fixture on the FIS Snowboard World Cup halfpipe circuit. He is also the co-founder of the Supernatural snowboard event, an invitational back-country freeriding competition held in British Columbia that has become one of the most respected alternative events in snowboarding. Davis is known for pushing halfpipe riding in an artistic and progressive direction, often forgoing safe competition strategies in favor of creative expression.

## Career Progression
- **2005:** Turned professional at age 16. Began competing on the FIS Snowboard World Cup halfpipe circuit.
- **2007–2010:** Won multiple X Games Aspen halfpipe medals. Established himself as a top World Cup halfpipe competitor with podiums across the US and Europe.
- **2011–2013:** Won X Games Aspen halfpipe gold. Competed at the FIS World Championships. Founded the Supernatural invitational with partner Eric Willett.
- **2014:** Won Olympic bronze in halfpipe at the 2014 Sochi Winter Olympics — his first and only Olympic medal. The bronze was notable given his unorthodox, style-first approach in a scoring environment that typically rewards technical trick difficulty.
- **2015–2018:** Competed at X Games Aspen and on the FIS World Cup circuit while maintaining involvement in the Supernatural event. Mentored younger riders on the US Team.
- **2018:** Competed at Pyeongchang Winter Olympics, finishing 6th in halfpipe final. Continued to be a force on the halfpipe circuit through strong technical riding and crowd-favorite status.
- **2019–2023:** Remained competitive on the X Games and FIS circuits in a reduced schedule. Focused increasingly on the Supernatural event and snowboard film projects. Has competed at Mammoth Mountain Grand Prix events.

## Competition Circuit
Davis competes primarily in halfpipe:
- **X Games Aspen** (Buttermilk Mountain / Aspen Snowmass) — multiple gold and silver medals in halfpipe
- **Winter Olympic Games** — bronze medal (2014 Sochi, halfpipe)
- **FIS Snowboard World Cup** — halfpipe circuit, multiple podiums and wins
- **US Grand Prix** (Park City Mountain Resort, Mammoth Mountain) — regular competitor
- **Dew Tour** (Copper Mountain) — halfpipe medalist
- **Supernatural** (co-founded; Baldface Lodge, BC) — organizer and competitor

## Sponsorships
Burton Snowboards (primary), Red Bull, Vans, Capita, Oakley

## Performance Notes
Davis's riding is characterized by exceptional amplitude, flowing body position, and a commitment to executing spins in both directions with equal comfort. He is known for Cab 1080s and frontside 900s performed at heights that few competitors match. He trains primarily at Mammoth Mountain, where he benefits from the resort's premier halfpipe facilities maintained for the US Ski & Snowboard halfpipe program. His competitive longevity — nearly two decades at the top level — is a testament to his commitment to progression and physical maintenance.
