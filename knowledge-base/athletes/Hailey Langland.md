# Athlete Profile: Hailey Langland

## Summary
- **Discipline(s):** Slopestyle Snowboard, Halfpipe Snowboard, Big Air Snowboard
- **Hometown:** San Clemente, California
- **Home Resort:** Mammoth Mountain, California
- **Born:** August 27, 2000
- **Turned Pro:** 2016
- **Stance:** Regular

## Career Highlights
Hailey Langland is one of the most dynamic multi-discipline snowboarders in the USA Snowboard program. She burst onto the scene as a teenager, winning X Games Aspen slopestyle gold in 2016 — making her one of the youngest X Games gold medalists in women's snowboard history. Langland has since developed into a credible competitor in halfpipe as well as slopestyle, giving her one of the widest competition footprints of any US woman. She has competed at two Winter Olympics and has multiple FIS Snowboard World Cup podiums to her name.

## Career Progression
- **2015–2016:** Emerged as a teenage prodigy on the junior and senior FIS slopestyle circuit. Won X Games Aspen slopestyle gold in January 2016 — her breakthrough moment on the global stage.
- **2017–2018:** Named to the US Olympic Team for Pyeongchang. Competed at the Pyeongchang Winter Olympics in slopestyle. Began developing halfpipe as a second discipline under the US Ski & Snowboard program at Mammoth Mountain.
- **2019:** Won FIS Snowboard World Cup slopestyle event. Podiumed in halfpipe at multiple events. Won bronze at the Dew Tour at Copper Mountain.
- **2020–2021:** Competed across slopestyle and halfpipe on the FIS World Cup circuit. Won X Games Aspen slopestyle silver. Trained halfpipe at Mammoth Mountain facilities alongside US team teammates.
- **2022:** Competed at the Beijing Winter Olympics in slopestyle. Won silver at the US Grand Prix at Park City Mountain Resort. Won multiple World Cup slopestyle podiums.
- **2023:** Continued competing across slopestyle and halfpipe. Won X Games Aspen slopestyle bronze. Named one of the athletes most likely to contend for multiple disciplines in the 2026 Milan-Cortina Olympic cycle.

## Competition Circuit
Langland competes across slopestyle, halfpipe, and big air:
- **X Games Aspen** (Buttermilk Mountain / Aspen Snowmass) — gold in slopestyle (2016), multiple medals
- **Winter Olympic Games** — two-time Olympian (2018 Pyeongchang, 2022 Beijing)
- **FIS Snowboard World Cup** — slopestyle and halfpipe circuits, multiple podiums
- **Dew Tour** (Copper Mountain) — multiple medals across disciplines
- **US Grand Prix** (Park City Mountain Resort) — regular competitor and podium finisher

## Sponsorships
Roxy, Red Bull, Oakley, Volcom

## Performance Notes
Langland's versatility across three disciplines is unusual even among elite snowboarders. Her slopestyle riding is distinguished by powerful backside spins and creative rail combinations; her halfpipe riding has steadily developed toward triple-digit scores in FIS competition. Growing up in Southern California and moving to Mammoth Mountain as her primary training base, she benefits from some of the best maintained park and pipe facilities in North America. She is coached under both the slopestyle and halfpipe programs within US Ski & Snowboard.
