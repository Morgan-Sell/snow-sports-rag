# Athlete Profile: Brita Sigourney

## Summary
- **Discipline(s):** Halfpipe Freeski
- **Hometown:** Carmel, California
- **Home Resort:** Mammoth Mountain, California
- **Born:** September 25, 1990
- **Turned Pro:** 2010
- **Stance:** Regular

## Career Highlights
Brita Sigourney is one of the most experienced and decorated women's halfpipe freeskiers in US history. She is a three-time Olympian (2014, 2018, 2022) and a bronze medalist at the 2018 Pyeongchang Winter Olympics — the first Olympic women's freeski halfpipe event. She has won multiple X Games Aspen halfpipe medals over a career spanning more than a decade of elite competition. Sigourney is known for her powerful skiing and competitive consistency, and has served as a veteran leader in the US Ski & Snowboard women's halfpipe program alongside younger teammates.

## Career Progression
- **2010:** Turned professional. Began competing on the nascent women's halfpipe freeski circuit.
- **2011–2013:** Won multiple FIS Freeski World Cup halfpipe events. Won X Games Aspen halfpipe silver. Became one of the top women's halfpipe freeskiers in the world as the discipline grew in prominence.
- **2014:** Competed at the Sochi Winter Olympics, finishing 6th in the first Olympic women's freeski halfpipe event. Won X Games Aspen halfpipe gold.
- **2015–2017:** Won multiple X Games Aspen halfpipe medals. Won FIS Freeski World Cup halfpipe events. Won Dew Tour halfpipe at Copper Mountain.
- **2018:** Won Olympic bronze in halfpipe freeski at the Pyeongchang Winter Olympics — one of the most important results of her career. Won X Games Aspen halfpipe silver.
- **2019–2021:** Continued to compete at the top level. Won multiple World Cup podiums. Won X Games Aspen halfpipe bronze. Mentored younger US halfpipe athletes including Hailey Langland.
- **2022:** Competed at the Beijing Winter Olympics, finishing in the top 10. Continued as a World Cup competitor and X Games participant.
- **2023:** Remained active on the X Games and FIS World Cup circuits. Training and competing from Mammoth Mountain.

## Competition Circuit
Sigourney competes exclusively in halfpipe freeski:
- **X Games Aspen** (Buttermilk Mountain / Aspen Snowmass) — multiple halfpipe medals including gold
- **Winter Olympic Games** — three-time Olympian (2014 Sochi, 2018 Pyeongchang, 2022 Beijing)
- **FIS Freeski World Cup** — halfpipe circuit, multiple wins and season podiums
- **Dew Tour** (Copper Mountain) — multiple halfpipe medals
- **US Grand Prix** (Mammoth Mountain, Park City Mountain Resort) — multiple wins

## Sponsorships
Rossignol (primary), Red Bull, Oakley, Patagonia

## Performance Notes
Sigourney's halfpipe skiing is defined by progressive technical content performed with exceptional amplitude and clean landings. She executes double corks in both rotational directions and has been at the forefront of technical progression in women's halfpipe freeski throughout her career. She trains at Mammoth Mountain, which operates one of the premier halfpipe facilities in the US under the US Ski & Snowboard team program. Her longevity at the elite level — competing at the top tier for over a decade — is a model of athletic maintenance and competitive discipline.
