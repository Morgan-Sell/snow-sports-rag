# Athlete Profile: Chris Corning

## Summary
- **Discipline(s):** Big Air Snowboard, Slopestyle Snowboard
- **Hometown:** Silverthorne, Colorado
- **Home Resort:** Breckenridge Ski Resort, Colorado
- **Born:** May 23, 1999
- **Turned Pro:** 2017
- **Stance:** Regular

## Career Highlights
Chris Corning is one of the biggest, most powerful jumpers in the history of competitive snowboarding. At 6'3", he generates exceptional air time that gives him more hangtime than virtually any competitor, enabling trick executions — particularly triple corks — that other riders cannot physically match. He was the first snowboarder to land a backside triple cork 1440 in FIS World Cup competition. Corning is a multiple FIS Snowboard World Cup big air and slopestyle winner, a two-time Olympian, and a regular X Games Aspen medalist. He grew up in Silverthorne, Colorado alongside Red Gerard, and the two have trained together at Breckenridge Ski Resort throughout their careers.

## Career Progression
- **2017:** Turned professional. Won FIS Snowboard Junior World Championships in big air and slopestyle. Named to the US Olympic Team for the 2018 Pyeongchang Games.
- **2018:** Competed at Pyeongchang Winter Olympics, finishing 6th in slopestyle. In the same season became the first snowboarder to land a backside triple cork 1440 in World Cup competition at a US Grand Prix event at Mammoth Mountain.
- **2019:** Won FIS Snowboard World Cup big air globe (season overall title). Won X Games Aspen big air silver. Became the dominant force in big air on the World Cup circuit.
- **2020–2021:** Won multiple FIS Snowboard World Cup big air and slopestyle events. Won X Games Aspen big air bronze. Competed at Dew Tour at Copper Mountain.
- **2022:** Competed at the Beijing Winter Olympics in slopestyle and big air. Won X Games Aspen big air silver. Continued consistent World Cup podiums across both disciplines.
- **2023:** Won X Games Aspen big air silver. Multiple FIS World Cup big air podiums. Trained extensively at Breckenridge and Copper Mountain.

## Competition Circuit
Corning competes primarily in big air with slopestyle as a secondary:
- **X Games Aspen** (Buttermilk Mountain / Aspen Snowmass) — multiple big air medals
- **Winter Olympic Games** — two-time Olympian (2018 Pyeongchang, 2022 Beijing)
- **FIS Snowboard World Cup** — big air globe winner, multiple slopestyle wins
- **US Grand Prix** (Park City Mountain Resort, Mammoth Mountain) — event winner
- **Dew Tour** (Copper Mountain) — regular competitor and medalist

## Sponsorships
Burton Snowboards (primary), Monster Energy, Oakley, Nixon

## Performance Notes
Corning's physical frame gives him a unique competitive advantage: more air time per jump means more time to complete rotations and add complexity to grabs. His backside triple cork 1440 has become his signature trick in competition. He trains at Breckenridge Ski Resort near his family home and regularly accesses Copper Mountain for the Dew Tour-spec jump features maintained there. Corning works with the US Ski & Snowboard big air and slopestyle coaching staff and has been mentored in part by his more experienced teammate Red Gerard throughout their overlapping careers in Silverthorne.
