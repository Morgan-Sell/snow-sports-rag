# Athlete Profile: Jamie Anderson

## Summary
- **Discipline(s):** Slopestyle Snowboard, Big Air Snowboard
- **Hometown:** South Lake Tahoe, California
- **Home Resort:** Palisades Tahoe, California
- **Born:** September 13, 1990
- **Turned Pro:** 2006
- **Stance:** Regular

## Career Highlights
Jamie Anderson is the most decorated women's slopestyle snowboarder in Olympic history. She won back-to-back Olympic gold medals in snowboard slopestyle at the 2014 Sochi Winter Olympics and the 2018 Pyeongchang Winter Olympics, making her the first snowboarder of any gender to win consecutive Olympic slopestyle titles. Anderson has won a record number of X Games medals in women's snowboard slopestyle, including multiple gold medals at X Games Aspen across more than a decade of competition. She is also a multiple FIS Snowboard World Cup slopestyle champion and has been the most consistent top-level performer in the discipline across the past 15 years.

## Career Progression
- **2006:** Turned professional at age 15. Immediately began competing on the FIS Snowboard World Cup slopestyle circuit.
- **2007–2010:** Built an extraordinary World Cup record with consistent wins and podiums across slopestyle events in the US and Europe. Established herself as the world's top women's slopestyle rider.
- **2011–2013:** Won multiple X Games Aspen slopestyle gold medals. Won FIS Snowboard World Championships slopestyle gold in Stoneham, Quebec.
- **2014:** Won Olympic gold in snowboard slopestyle at the 2014 Sochi Winter Olympics — the first time the discipline was included in the Olympics. Won with a score of 95.25, a massive margin over the field.
- **2015–2017:** Continued dominance on the World Cup and X Games circuits. Won additional X Games Aspen slopestyle golds. Began competing in big air as the discipline entered the Olympic program.
- **2018:** Won Olympic gold in snowboard slopestyle at the Pyeongchang Winter Olympics, becoming the first snowboarder to win consecutive Olympic slopestyle titles. Also competed in the inaugural Olympic big air event, finishing fourth.
- **2019–2021:** Maintained World Cup competitiveness while managing injuries. Won multiple Dew Tour medals at Copper Mountain.
- **2022:** Competed at the Beijing Winter Olympics in slopestyle and big air, finishing outside the podium but as one of the most experienced competitors in both fields. Continued competing on the X Games and World Cup circuits.
- **2023:** Won X Games Aspen slopestyle bronze. Remained a top-15 World Cup competitor and active member of the US Ski & Snowboard team.

## Competition Circuit
Anderson competes across slopestyle and big air disciplines:
- **X Games Aspen** (Buttermilk Mountain / Aspen Snowmass) — record multiple slopestyle gold medals
- **Winter Olympic Games** — two-time champion (2014 Sochi, 2018 Pyeongchang)
- **FIS Snowboard World Cup** — slopestyle circuit, multiple season titles
- **Dew Tour** (Copper Mountain) — consistent medalist
- **US Grand Prix** (Park City Mountain Resort) — multiple wins
- **Burton US Open Snowboarding Championships** (Stratton Mountain, VT) — podium finisher

## Sponsorships
Burton Snowboards (primary), Red Bull, Jeep, Oakley, Roxy, and additional lifestyle brand partners

## Performance Notes
Anderson is renowned for her smooth, flowing riding style and ability to string together creative trick combinations across both rail and jump features. She is a master of front-side spins and executes 900s and 1080s with exceptional amplitude and style. A South Lake Tahoe native, she grew up riding at Palisades Tahoe and maintains a close connection to that resort as her primary training base. She is known for her connection to nature and mindfulness practices as part of her athletic preparation, a philosophy that sets her apart from many peers on the competition circuit.
