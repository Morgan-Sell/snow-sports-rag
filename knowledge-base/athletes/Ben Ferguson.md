# Athlete Profile: Ben Ferguson

## Summary
- **Discipline(s):** Halfpipe Snowboard, Slopestyle Snowboard
- **Hometown:** Bend, Oregon
- **Home Resort:** Mount Bachelor, Oregon
- **Born:** December 30, 1996
- **Turned Pro:** 2015
- **Stance:** Regular

## Career Highlights
Ben Ferguson is one of the most versatile pipe-and-park snowboarders on the US Team, equally capable of competing at the elite level in both halfpipe and slopestyle. He is a multiple X Games Aspen medalist in halfpipe and has made the Olympic halfpipe final at both the 2018 Pyeongchang and 2022 Beijing Games. Ferguson is known for his powerful, creative riding style — particularly his emphasis on left-spin tricks and a distinctive visual flair — and for his involvement in snowboard film projects that have elevated his profile beyond the contest circuit.

## Career Progression
- **2015:** Turned professional. Immediately began competing in FIS Snowboard World Cup events in halfpipe and began developing slopestyle as a second discipline.
- **2016–2017:** Won X Games Aspen halfpipe silver. Multiple FIS World Cup halfpipe podiums. Named to the US Olympic Team for Pyeongchang.
- **2018:** Competed at the Pyeongchang Winter Olympics, finishing 5th in halfpipe. Continued to refine technical content with new left-side trick combinations.
- **2019–2021:** Won X Games Aspen halfpipe bronze. Multiple World Cup podiums in halfpipe. Starred in snowboard film projects including those produced by the Arcade collective.
- **2022:** Competed at the Beijing Winter Olympics, reaching the halfpipe final. Won X Games Aspen halfpipe silver — his best X Games result. Podiumed at multiple FIS Snowboard World Cup halfpipe events.
- **2023:** Won X Games Aspen halfpipe silver. Competed on full FIS World Cup halfpipe and slopestyle circuits. Continued to be one of the most technically consistent US halfpipe athletes.

## Competition Circuit
Ferguson competes in halfpipe as his primary discipline, with slopestyle as a secondary:
- **X Games Aspen** (Buttermilk Mountain / Aspen Snowmass) — multiple halfpipe medals
- **Winter Olympic Games** — competed (2018 Pyeongchang, 2022 Beijing)
- **FIS Snowboard World Cup** — halfpipe circuit, multiple podiums
- **US Grand Prix** (Park City Mountain Resort) — regular competitor
- **Dew Tour** (Copper Mountain) — halfpipe medalist
- **Burton US Open Snowboarding Championships** (Stratton Mountain, VT) — top-five finishes

## Sponsorships
Burton Snowboards (primary), Red Bull, Oakley, Volcom, Vans

## Performance Notes
Ferguson is distinguished in the halfpipe by his commitment to left-side spins — Cab variations and switch runs — which he executes with remarkable power and amplitude. He consistently lands Cab 1080s and frontside 1080s at heights exceeding 18 feet above the pipe lip. Growing up at Mount Bachelor in Oregon, Ferguson developed his technical foundation on a comparatively lower-altitude mountain than most elite halfpipe riders, which coaches credit for building exceptional all-terrain adaptability. He trains seasonally at Mammoth Mountain for US Ski & Snowboard team halfpipe camps.
