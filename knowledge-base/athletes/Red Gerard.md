# Athlete Profile: Red Gerard

## Summary
- **Discipline(s):** Slopestyle Snowboard, Big Air Snowboard
- **Hometown:** Silverthorne, Colorado
- **Home Resort:** Breckenridge Ski Resort, Colorado
- **Born:** June 29, 2000
- **Turned Pro:** 2017
- **Stance:** Regular

## Career Highlights
Red Gerard became a national sports story at age 17 when he won the snowboard slopestyle gold medal at the 2018 Pyeongchang Winter Olympics — the first US gold medal of those Games — after barely waking up in time for competition and borrowing a teammate's jacket. His casual demeanor, youth, and technical brilliance made him an instant fan favorite. Gerard is one of the most technically inventive slopestyle and big air riders in the world, consistently pushing the boundary of trick combinations on rails and jumps. He has multiple X Games medals and FIS World Cup podiums, and is widely considered the future of USA Snowboard slopestyle.

## Career Progression
- **2016:** At age 15, competed in first professional FIS events and showed immediate promise on the World Cup slopestyle circuit.
- **2017:** Won his first FIS Snowboard World Cup slopestyle event. Named to the US Olympic Team for Pyeongchang at age 17.
- **2018:** Won Olympic gold in snowboard slopestyle at the Pyeongchang Winter Olympics with a final-run score of 87.16, overcoming two fall-heavy earlier runs to land a near-perfect third run. The youngest American man to win a Winter Olympic gold medal. Followed the Olympic win with multiple X Games Aspen medals.
- **2019:** Competed on the full FIS Snowboard World Cup slopestyle circuit. Won silver at X Games Aspen slopestyle. Began developing big air as a secondary discipline.
- **2020–2021:** Continued top World Cup results across slopestyle and big air. Won multiple Dew Tour medals at Copper Mountain.
- **2022:** Competed at the Beijing Winter Olympics, finishing 7th in slopestyle. Rebounded with X Games Aspen slopestyle bronze. Focused on building more consistent big air runs.
- **2023:** Won X Games Aspen big air gold, his first X Games gold in that discipline. Podiumed at multiple FIS Snowboard World Cup events across slopestyle and big air. Trained extensively at Breckenridge and Mammoth Mountain.

## Competition Circuit
Gerard competes primarily in slopestyle and big air across these events:
- **X Games Aspen** (Buttermilk Mountain / Aspen Snowmass) — multiple slopestyle and big air medals
- **Winter Olympic Games** — gold medal (2018 Pyeongchang)
- **FIS Snowboard World Cup** — slopestyle and big air circuits, multiple podiums
- **Dew Tour** (Copper Mountain) — consistent podium finisher
- **US Grand Prix** (Park City Mountain Resort) — qualifier and competitor

## Sponsorships
Volcom (primary apparel), Oakley, Monster Energy, Skullcandy, GNU Snowboards

## Performance Notes
Gerard's riding style is characterized by creative trick selection, casual execution, and a fearless approach to high-consequence jumps. He regularly incorporates front and backside triple corks on jump features and has one of the most diverse trick bags of any active slopestyle competitor. He trains at Breckenridge Ski Resort near his family home in Silverthorne and travels to Mammoth Mountain and Copper Mountain for specific feature and halfpipe development. Gerard is coached through the US Ski & Snowboard slopestyle program.
