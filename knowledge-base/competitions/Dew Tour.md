# Competition: Dew Tour

## Overview
- **Sanctioning Body:** FIS (FIS Snowboard and Freeski World Cup event)
- **Disciplines:** Snowboard Halfpipe (W/M), Snowboard Slopestyle (W/M), Snowboard Big Air (W/M), Freeski Halfpipe (W/M), Freeski Slopestyle (W/M), Street/Rail disciplines
- **Frequency:** Annual (December)
- **Venue:** Copper Mountain Resort, Colorado
- **Prize Money:** Competitive professional prize purse
- **FIS Points:** Awarded (FIS Snowboard and Freeski World Cup event)

## Format & Judging
Dew Tour uses FIS judging criteria across all disciplines. Unique to the Dew Tour is its inclusion of street/rail-focused events that prioritize technical jib riding over amplitude-based jump tricks — a format that showcases a different set of athlete skills than halfpipe or traditional slopestyle. Halfpipe and slopestyle formats follow standard FIS protocols. The December timing at Copper Mountain makes this one of the earliest elite events of each competitive season.

## Significance
As an FIS-sanctioned event, Dew Tour results directly impact Olympic qualification rankings. The early-season timing at Copper Mountain means athletes use it as a season-opening form check, and strong Dew Tour performances set the tone for the year. Copper Mountain's investment in competition infrastructure — including the Woodward training facility — makes it one of the best-prepared venues on the US calendar.

## Olympic / FIS Qualifier Status
Dew Tour is a full FIS World Cup event for both snowboard and freeski disciplines. Results count directly toward Olympic qualification rankings. Its December timing makes it one of the first meaningful qualification data points of each Olympic qualifying cycle.
