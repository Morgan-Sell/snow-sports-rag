# Competition: FIS Alpine Ski World Cup

## Overview
- **Sanctioning Body:** FIS (Fédération Internationale de Ski)
- **Disciplines:** Slalom (SL), Giant Slalom (GS), Super-G (SG), Downhill (DH), Alpine Combined, Parallel events
- **Frequency:** Annual circuit (October–March)
- **Venues:** Rotating international venues; US events at Killington Resort (VT) and Beaver Creek (CO)
- **Prize Money:** FIS-standardized prize structure; among the largest in Olympic winter sport
- **FIS Points:** Primary ranking system; World Cup globe per discipline per season

## Format & Rules
Alpine ski racing is a timed discipline — the athlete with the lowest combined time across one or two runs wins. Slalom and GS use two runs on separate courses (or different sets of gates on the same slope); the combined time determines results. Super-G and Downhill are single-run events. Athletes who miss a gate are disqualified. The FIS Alpine World Cup circuit runs approximately October through March, with 30–40 events across Europe, North America, and occasional overseas venues.

## US Venues
- **Killington Resort, Vermont** — Hosts the annual Killington World Cup in slalom (and GS in some years), typically in late November or December. The event draws the largest crowds of any FIS Alpine World Cup event in North America, largely due to Mikaela Shiffrin's participation.
- **Beaver Creek / Birds of Prey, Colorado** — Hosts the annual Birds of Prey FIS Alpine World Cup, featuring downhill, super-G, and GS events for men. One of the most storied speed race venues in the world.

## US Champions — Season Globe Winners (Selected)
- **Slalom Women:** Mikaela Shiffrin — multiple overall and slalom globe winner; arguably the greatest slalom skier in history
- **GS Women:** Mikaela Shiffrin — multiple GS globe wins
- **GS Men:** Ted Ligety — multiple GS globes (retired 2019)
- **Super-G Men:** Ryan Cochran-Siegle — World Cup event winner; rising globe contender
- **GS Men (active):** Tommy Ford — World Cup event winner; top US GS contender

## Olympic / FIS Qualifier Status
The FIS Alpine Ski World Cup IS the Olympic qualifying system for alpine racing. World Cup ranking points determine national quota allocations. US athletes including Mikaela Shiffrin, Tommy Ford, Nina O'Brien, and Ryan Cochran-Siegle qualify for Olympics through World Cup standing.
