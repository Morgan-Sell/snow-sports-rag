# Competition: X Games Aspen

## Overview
- **Sanctioning Body:** ESPN / ABC (not FIS-sanctioned; independent)
- **Disciplines:** Snowboard Halfpipe (W/M), Snowboard Slopestyle (W/M), Snowboard Big Air (W/M), Freeski Halfpipe (W/M), Freeski Slopestyle (W/M), Freeski Big Air (W/M)
- **Frequency:** Annual (January)
- **Venue:** Buttermilk Mountain, Aspen Snowmass, Colorado
- **Prize Money:** Approximately $1,000,000 total purse across all events
- **FIS Points:** Not awarded (non-FIS event)

## Format & Judging
Each X Games discipline uses a judge panel scoring system. Athletes typically receive two or three runs in qualification and finals, with the best single run score counting. Halfpipe is scored on amplitude, difficulty, variety of tricks, execution, and progression. Slopestyle is scored on the quality of execution across all rail and jump features, with deductions for falls and style penalties. Big air is scored on difficulty and execution of a single trick. Scores are presented on a 100-point scale.

## Olympic / FIS Qualifier Status
X Games Aspen is not an FIS-sanctioned event and does not award Olympic qualifying points. However, it is widely regarded as a de facto performance indicator for the Olympic season and is often cited in US Ski & Snowboard team selection deliberations as evidence of competitive form.
