# Competition: Burton US Open Snowboarding Championships

## Overview
- **Sanctioning Body:** Burton Snowboards / FIS (FIS-sanctioned World Cup event)
- **Disciplines:** Snowboard Halfpipe (W/M), Snowboard Slopestyle (W/M)
- **Frequency:** Annual (March)
- **Venue:** Stratton Mountain Resort, Vermont
- **Prize Money:** One of the richest prize purses in professional snowboarding
- **FIS Points:** Awarded (FIS Snowboard World Cup event)

## Format & Judging
The Burton US Open uses FIS judging criteria with a panel of five judges. Halfpipe athletes receive three runs in finals; the best single run score counts. Slopestyle athletes receive two or three runs, best run scoring. The event features a distinctive festival atmosphere that has made it one of the most athlete-beloved events on the calendar, with a reputation for exceptional course-building and a relaxed, community-oriented event culture that reflects Burton's snowboard heritage.

## Historical Significance
The Burton US Open is the oldest and most historically significant professional snowboard competition in the world, having been held since 1982. Burton Snowboards organized the event from the beginning as a celebration of snowboard culture as much as a competition. Many of snowboarding's foundational figures competed at the US Open before the sport achieved FIS and Olympic recognition.

## Olympic / FIS Qualifier Status
The Burton US Open is a full FIS Snowboard World Cup event. Results award FIS World Cup ranking points that count directly toward Olympic qualification standings. It is typically held in March, late in the qualification period, meaning results at the US Open can be pivotal for athletes on the bubble of Olympic team selection.
