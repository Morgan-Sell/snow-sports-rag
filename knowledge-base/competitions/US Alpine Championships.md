# Competition: US Alpine Ski Championships

## Overview
- **Sanctioning Body:** US Ski & Snowboard (USSS)
- **Disciplines:** Slalom (SL), Giant Slalom (GS), Super-G (SG), Downhill (DH), Alpine Combined
- **Frequency:** Annual (March–April)
- **Venue:** Sun Valley Resort, Idaho (primary and most frequent host)
- **Prize Money:** Domestic competition prize structure
- **FIS Points:** Not awarded (domestic event only)

## Format & Rules
The US Alpine Ski Championships follow FIS race format standards — timed runs on gate-set courses, with combined times determining results across two-run events (SL, GS) or single-run events (SG, DH). The championships serve as the definitive domestic title in US alpine ski racing. Winning the US title carries significant prestige even when FIS points are not at stake, as it represents the pinnacle of the domestic ranking system.

## Significance
The US Alpine Championships at Sun Valley is the most storied domestic alpine race event in the United States. Sun Valley's Bald Mountain (Baldy) provides ideal race terrain with sustained vertical and consistent fall lines. Multiple generations of US alpine champions have been crowned here, from Phil Mahre and Tamara McKinney to Ted Ligety and Mikaela Shiffrin. The event is typically used by US athletes as the final domestic competition of the season and a tune-up before World Cup Finals in Europe.

## Historical US Champions (Selected)

### Slalom / Giant Slalom — Women
- **Multiple years:** Mikaela Shiffrin — dominant across technical disciplines; multiple national titles at Sun Valley
- **2023:** Mikaela Shiffrin (SL, GS)
- **2022:** Nina O'Brien — GS silver at US Championships; notable result before Beijing Olympics

### GS / Super-G — Men
- **Multiple years:** Ted Ligety — dominated GS at US Championships throughout his career (retired 2019)
- **2022–2023:** Tommy Ford — GS national champion
- **2022–2023:** Ryan Cochran-Siegle — super-G national champion

## Olympic / FIS Qualifier Status
The US Alpine Championships are not FIS-sanctioned and do not award Olympic qualification points. However, USSS considers national championship results in team selection decisions, particularly in borderline cases where multiple athletes have similar World Cup rankings.
