# Competition: FIS Snowboard World Cup

## Overview
- **Sanctioning Body:** FIS (Fédération Internationale de Ski)
- **Disciplines:** Halfpipe (W/M), Slopestyle (W/M), Big Air (W/M), Parallel Giant Slalom, Snowboard Cross
- **Frequency:** Annual circuit (November–March)
- **Venues:** Rotating international venues; US events at Park City Mountain Resort and Mammoth Mountain
- **Prize Money:** FIS-standardized prize structure per event
- **FIS Points:** Primary ranking system; World Cup globe awarded to season overall winner per discipline

## Format & Judging
FIS Snowboard World Cup events in halfpipe, slopestyle, and big air use standardized judging panels of five judges scoring on amplitude, difficulty, execution, variety, and progression. Athletes receive two or three runs in finals; the best single run counts. The season-long circuit awards points on a scale from 100 (first place) to lower values for each subsequent finishing position. The athlete with the most accumulated points across all events in a discipline wins the World Cup globe — the sport's most prestigious season-long honor outside of Olympic and World Championship medals.

## Significance for US Athletes
The FIS Snowboard World Cup circuit is the primary route to Olympic qualification and the most meaningful measure of sustained competitive excellence across a full season. Chloe Kim has won multiple World Cup halfpipe globes, making her the benchmark US World Cup performer. Red Gerard, Julia Marino, Jamie Anderson, Chris Corning, and Kyle Mack are regular World Cup podium contenders in slopestyle and big air.

## US Champions — Season Globe Winners (Selected)
- **Halfpipe Women:** Chloe Kim — multiple season globe wins
- **Slopestyle Men:** Red Gerard — podium finishes; Chris Corning — Big Air globe winner (2019)
- **Slopestyle Women:** Julia Marino — consistent World Cup podium performer

## Olympic / FIS Qualifier Status
The FIS Snowboard World Cup IS the Olympic qualifying system. Ranking points accumulated across World Cup events during the two seasons preceding the Games determine Olympic quota allocations per nation and athlete eligibility. The US Grand Prix and Burton US Open are individual World Cup events within this circuit.
