# Competition: Winter Olympic Games

## Overview
- **Sanctioning Body:** International Olympic Committee (IOC) / FIS
- **Disciplines (relevant):** Snowboard Halfpipe, Snowboard Slopestyle, Snowboard Big Air, Freeski Halfpipe, Freeski Slopestyle, Freeski Big Air, Alpine Ski Racing (SL, GS, SG, DH, Combined)
- **Frequency:** Every 4 years
- **Recent Venues:** Pyeongchang, South Korea (2018); Beijing, China (2022); Milan-Cortina, Italy (2026)
- **Prize Money:** No prize money (Olympic tradition); medals only
- **FIS Points:** Not awarded (separate Olympic competition)

## Format & Significance
The Winter Olympic Games are the most prestigious competition in the world for athletes in all snow sports disciplines. Olympic medals represent the pinnacle of athletic achievement and carry cultural, commercial, and historical weight that no other competition can match. For snowboard and freeski athletes, Olympic gold is the ultimate career achievement. For alpine ski racers, Olympic success alongside World Cup dominance defines the historical ranking of greatness.

## Olympic / Qualification Pathway
Athletes qualify for the Winter Olympics through FIS World Cup rankings accumulated in the two seasons preceding the Games. US Ski & Snowboard selects the US Olympic Team from eligible athletes, typically in December before the Games. The next Winter Olympics — Milan-Cortina 2026 — represents the primary goal for current active US athletes.
