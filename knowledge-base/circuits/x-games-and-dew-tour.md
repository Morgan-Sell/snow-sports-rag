# X Games & Dew Tour: North American Non-FIS Competition Circuit

## X Games Aspen

### Overview
X Games Aspen is the flagship action sports competition in the world. Produced by ESPN and ABC, it is held each January at Buttermilk Mountain within the Aspen Snowmass resort complex. X Games Aspen features snowboard halfpipe, snowboard slopestyle, snowboard big air, freeski halfpipe, freeski slopestyle, and freeski big air disciplines across men's and women's fields. The event is broadcast to a global audience and carries prize money and sponsorship value that rivals or exceeds FIS World Cup events.

### Significance
X Games gold is considered by many in the snowboard and freeski community to be as prestigious as an Olympic medal — and in some disciplines, more difficult to win given the depth of the field and the stakes. Shaun White's 13 X Games halfpipe gold medals and Jamie Anderson's multiple slopestyle golds are among the most celebrated records in action sports. X Games Aspen has been held at Buttermilk Mountain since 2002.

### Relationship to FIS
X Games is not an FIS-sanctioned event and does not award FIS ranking points. However, FIS rules were adjusted to permit athletes on national teams to compete at X Games without jeopardizing their FIS eligibility, recognizing the event's cultural importance to the sport.

## Dew Tour

### Overview
The Dew Tour is an annual snowboard and freeski competition event held at Copper Mountain, Colorado, in December. It serves as an important early-season event on the US competitive calendar and is sanctioned by FIS, meaning it awards World Cup ranking points and counts toward Olympic qualification. The Dew Tour features halfpipe, slopestyle, and street/rail disciplines, and is broadcast on NBC Sports and streaming platforms.

### Significance
The Dew Tour's December timing at Copper Mountain makes it one of the first elite events of each competition season, providing an important early-season performance indicator. Its FIS sanctioning distinguishes it from X Games in that Dew Tour results directly affect Olympic qualification standings. US athletes including Chloe Kim, Red Gerard, Julia Marino, Nick Goepper, Alex Hall, Maggie Voisin, and Kyle Mack are regular competitors.
