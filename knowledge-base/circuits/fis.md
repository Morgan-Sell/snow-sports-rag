# FIS (Fédération Internationale de Ski)

## Organization Profile
The Fédération Internationale de Ski (FIS) is the international governing body for skiing and snowboarding competition worldwide. Founded in 1924 and headquartered in Oberhofen, Switzerland, FIS governs alpine skiing, Nordic skiing, ski jumping, freestyle skiing, snowboarding, and telemark skiing at the World Cup, World Championship, and Olympic levels. FIS rules and ranking systems govern Olympic qualification for all snowboard and freeski disciplines.

## Relevant Circuits
- **FIS Alpine Ski World Cup** — The premier annual alpine ski racing circuit, running from October through March with events across Europe, North America, and occasionally Asia. US venues include Killington Resort (Vermont) and Beaver Creek/Birds of Prey (Colorado).
- **FIS Snowboard World Cup** — Annual circuit covering halfpipe, slopestyle, and big air disciplines. US team athletes including Chloe Kim, Red Gerard, Julia Marino, Jamie Anderson, Chris Corning, and Kyle Mack compete on this circuit.
- **FIS Freeski World Cup** — Annual circuit covering halfpipe and slopestyle disciplines. US team athletes including Nick Goepper, Alex Hall, Maggie Voisin, Brita Sigourney, and Torin Yater-Wallace compete on this circuit.

## Olympic Qualification
FIS administers the Olympic qualification ranking system for all snowboard and freeski disciplines. Athletes accumulate FIS ranking points through World Cup results across the qualifying period (typically the two seasons preceding the Games). National Olympic Committees, including US Ski & Snowboard, use these rankings to select their teams within FIS quota allocations.

## World Championships
The FIS Alpine Ski World Championships and FIS Freestyle Ski and Snowboard World Championships are held every two years, in the odd years between Winter Olympics. These events are the second-most prestigious competitions after the Olympics and provide key ranking points and competitive development opportunities for US athletes.
