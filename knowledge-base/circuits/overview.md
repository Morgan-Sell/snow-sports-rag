# US Competitive Snowboard & Ski Landscape: Overview

## Introduction
The United States is one of the dominant nations in competitive snowboarding and freeskiing, with a robust governing structure, a deep athlete pipeline, and a network of world-class mountain venues. Competition in the US spans two broad tracks: the FIS (Fédération Internationale de Ski) international circuit, which governs Olympic qualification, and the North American event circuit anchored by X Games and the Dew Tour.

## Governing Body: US Ski & Snowboard (USSS)
US Ski & Snowboard is the national governing body (NGB) for Olympic skiing and snowboarding in the United States, headquartered in Park City, Utah. It oversees athlete development, national team selection, coaching programs, and domestic competition administration across alpine ski racing, freestyle skiing, freeski slopestyle and halfpipe, snowboard halfpipe, snowboard slopestyle, snowboard big air, and Nordic disciplines. The organization manages the US Ski & Snowboard High Performance Center at Park City Mountain Resort, which serves as the primary training hub for national team athletes in park and pipe disciplines.

## Disciplines Covered in This Knowledge Base
- **Snowboard Halfpipe** — Athletes perform aerial tricks in a U-shaped snow structure; scored by judges on amplitude, difficulty, and execution.
- **Snowboard Slopestyle** — Athletes descend a course featuring rails, boxes, and jump features, performing tricks on each; scored by judges.
- **Snowboard Big Air** — Athletes launch off a single large kicker and perform a trick; scored by judges.
- **Freeski Halfpipe** — Same structure as snowboard halfpipe but performed on skis.
- **Freeski Slopestyle** — Same structure as snowboard slopestyle but performed on skis.
- **Alpine Ski Racing** — Athletes race through a timed course of gates; disciplines include slalom (SL), giant slalom (GS), super-G (SG), and downhill (DH).

## Competition Ecosystem
The US competitive calendar integrates FIS World Cup events (internationally governed, Olympic qualifying), X Games (ESPN/ABC produced, prize-money driven), Dew Tour (NBC Sports, mid-season), Burton US Open (brand-organized, historically significant), and the US Grand Prix (USSS-organized Olympic qualifier). Athletes typically compete across 6–10 events per season.

## Olympic Pathway
Olympic qualification flows through FIS World Cup rankings for snowboard and freeski disciplines. The US Olympic Team is selected by US Ski & Snowboard based on FIS ranking points accumulated across the qualifying period, supplemented by domestic trials results at events such as the US Grand Prix at Park City Mountain Resort.
