# US Ski & Snowboard (USSS)

## Organization Profile
US Ski & Snowboard (USSS) is the national governing body for Olympic skiing and snowboarding in the United States. Headquartered in Park City, Utah, it is a member of the United States Olympic & Paralympic Committee (USOPC) and the international federation FIS (Fédération Internationale de Ski). USSS governs athlete development, team selection, coaching certification, and domestic event sanctioning across all Olympic ski and snowboard disciplines.

## High Performance Center
The US Ski & Snowboard High Performance Center operates primarily at Park City Mountain Resort, Utah. This facility hosts year-round training for national team athletes in halfpipe and slopestyle snowboard, halfpipe and slopestyle freeski, and alpine ski racing. The center operates superpipe and slopestyle venues maintained to FIS World Cup specifications and coordinates with Mammoth Mountain, Copper Mountain, and other partner resorts for broader team training operations.

## National Teams
USSS fields national teams across all disciplines. Athletes including Chloe Kim, Shaun White, Red Gerard, Julia Marino, Jamie Anderson, Danny Davis, Ben Ferguson, Chris Corning, Hailey Langland, Kyle Mack (snowboard), Nick Goepper, Alex Hall, Maggie Voisin, Brita Sigourney, Torin Yater-Wallace (freeski), and Mikaela Shiffrin, Ted Ligety, Tommy Ford, Nina O'Brien, Ryan Cochran-Siegle (alpine) have all represented USSS at the Olympic level.

## Domestic Event Oversight
USSS organizes and sanctions the US Grand Prix at Park City Mountain Resort, the primary domestic Olympic qualifying competition for snowboard and freeski park and pipe athletes. It also sanctions the US Alpine Championships held at Sun Valley, Idaho, and oversees selection and preparation for FIS World Championships and the Winter Olympic Games.

## Athlete Support
USSS provides funding, coaching, sports science support, and competition logistics for national team athletes across all disciplines. The organization's relationship with partner resorts — Park City Mountain Resort, Mammoth Mountain, Copper Mountain, Steamboat Springs — underpins the physical training infrastructure available to US athletes.
