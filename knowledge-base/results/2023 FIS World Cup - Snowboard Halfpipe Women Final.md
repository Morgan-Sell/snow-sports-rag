# 2023 FIS Snowboard World Cup — Halfpipe Women Season Final Results

## Event Details
- **Date:** March 18, 2023
- **Venue:** Silvaplana, Switzerland
- **Discipline:** FIS Snowboard World Cup — Halfpipe Women — Season Final
- **Conditions:** Variable alpine conditions; high altitude; firm pipe with challenging wind

## Season Globe Winner
**Chloe Kim** (USA) — FIS Snowboard World Cup Halfpipe Women Globe — Season Champion

## Final Event Podium
1. **Chloe Kim** (USA) — 91.50
2. Queralt Castellet (ESP) — 88.00
3. Kokomo Murase (JPN) — 85.75

## US Team Performance
**Chloe Kim** (USA) clinched her third FIS Snowboard World Cup halfpipe season globe by winning the season final event in Switzerland. Kim's dominance across the 2022–2023 FIS World Cup halfpipe circuit was comprehensive — she won or podiumed in every event she entered, producing the highest season-average score in women's halfpipe history.

Kim's season final run in Silvaplana featured back-to-back Cab 1080s at amplitude consistently above 20 feet — a run she described as her most complete under the most technically demanding conditions of the season. The high-altitude Swiss venue and variable winds made it the most technically challenging event of the circuit.

Kim acknowledged the season final from Mammoth Mountain (California), where she had returned for post-season training, expressing gratitude to US Ski & Snowboard and to the halfpipe coaching staff she credited for her sustained dominance.

**Hailey Langland** (USA) competed in the World Cup halfpipe circuit as a secondary discipline. Her season results reflected continued development with consistent top-10 World Cup placings.

## Season Overview
Chloe Kim's 2022–2023 FIS Snowboard World Cup season was her third globe-winning campaign. Combined with her 2022 Olympic gold and 2023 X Games Aspen gold, it made the 2022–2023 season the most decorated single season of any women's halfpipe snowboarder's career. She was named US Ski & Snowboard Athlete of the Year.
