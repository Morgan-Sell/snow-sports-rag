# 2022 Beijing Winter Olympics — Alpine Ski Super-G Men Results

## Event Details
- **Date:** February 7, 2022
- **Venue:** National Alpine Ski Center, Yanqing, China
- **Discipline:** Alpine Ski Racing — Super-G — Men
- **Conditions:** Cold and sunny; the Yanqing course was steep and technically demanding

1. Matthias Mayer (AUT) — 1:19.94
2. **Ryan Cochran-Siegle** (USA) — 1:20.04
3. Aleksander Aamodt Kilde (NOR) — 1:20.36

## US Team Performance
**Ryan Cochran-Siegle** (USA) won the Olympic silver medal in super-G in one of the most emotionally resonant results in recent US alpine ski racing history. Cochran-Siegle had undergone serious neck surgery following a crash in Beaver Creek in 2020 — injuries that many observers feared would end his career. Instead, less than two years later, he stood on the Olympic podium at the Beijing Games, just 0.10 seconds behind gold medalist Matthias Mayer of Austria.

Cochran-Siegle, whose home resort is Sugarbush Resort in Vermont and who grew up in a family steeped in ski racing tradition (his mother Barbara Cochran won Olympic slalom gold in 1972), trained for the Olympic super-G primarily with the US Ski & Snowboard alpine team program. His result was the best by a US male speed skier in over a decade and triggered immediate comparisons to his mother's historic victory.

The silver at Beijing came after multiple seasons of patient comeback work, FIS World Cup super-G podiums that built his ranking, and the courage to race full-speed on a technically demanding course with hardware in his neck. US Ski & Snowboard named Cochran-Siegle's performance the most inspiring result of the 2022 Olympic cycle.

## Historical Significance
Ryan Cochran-Siegle's silver medal represents one of the most compelling narratives in US alpine ski racing history — a neck surgery survivor becoming an Olympic medalist, from a ski racing family, in a discipline that demands total commitment to speed. The result validated the US men's alpine speed program and established Cochran-Siegle as the leader of that discipline for the coming Olympic cycle.
