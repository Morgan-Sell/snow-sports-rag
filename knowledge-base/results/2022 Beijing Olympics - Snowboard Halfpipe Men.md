# 2022 Beijing Winter Olympics — Snowboard Halfpipe Men Results

## Event Details
- **Date:** February 11, 2022
- **Venue:** Genting Snow Park, Zhangjiakou, China
- **Discipline:** Snowboard Halfpipe — Men
- **Conditions:** Bitterly cold (-20°C wind chill during finals); the pipe retained firm, fast walls throughout

## Podium
1. Ayumu Hirano (JPN) — 96.00
2. Jan Scherrer (SUI) — 87.25
3. Patrick Burgener (SUI) — 85.75

## US Team Performance
**Shaun White** (USA) finished 4th in his final Olympic halfpipe competition — one position from the podium. White's run included a back-to-back 1440 combination on his best attempt, but fell short of Hirano's extraordinary performance featuring a frontside triple cork 1440. The 4th-place finish was nevertheless a fitting bookend to a career that had produced three Olympic gold medals (2006, 2010, 2018). After the finals, White removed his helmet and delivered an emotional farewell to the halfpipe — a moment broadcast globally and widely regarded as one of the most poignant in action sports history.

White, who has trained at Park City Mountain Resort as his primary US base and competed in thousands of halfpipe sessions at Buttermilk Mountain at X Games Aspen, described his 2022 Olympic campaign as his most meaningful. He announced his retirement from competition at X Games Aspen 2022 weeks later.

**Ben Ferguson** (USA) also competed at the Beijing halfpipe, posting competitive runs but finishing outside the top eight in a deep field. Ferguson, who trains at Mammoth Mountain with the US halfpipe team, used the Olympic experience as a learning platform for the next Games cycle.

## Historical Significance
White's farewell at Beijing concluded the most decorated individual career in snowboard halfpipe history: three Olympic golds, 13 X Games Aspen gold medals, and over 80 World Cup victories. His 4th-place finish in his final Olympics captured the sport's imagination worldwide and triggered an outpouring of recognition across the global ski and snowboard community.
