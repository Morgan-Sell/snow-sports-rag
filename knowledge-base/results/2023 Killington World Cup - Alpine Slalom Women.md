# 2023 Killington World Cup — Alpine Ski Slalom Women Results

## Event Details
- **Date:** November 26, 2023
- **Venue:** Killington Resort, Vermont (Superstar trail)
- **Discipline:** Alpine Ski Racing — Slalom — Women
- **Conditions:** Cold and overcast; the Superstar trail was firm, well-set, and held through both runs

## Podium
1. **Mikaela Shiffrin** (USA) — 1:44.67 (combined two runs)
2. Wendy Holdener (SUI) — 1:45.23
3. Petra Vlhova (SVK) — 1:45.81

## US Team Performance
**Mikaela Shiffrin** (USA) won the Killington World Cup slalom for the fourth consecutive year, extending what has become one of the most dominant home-event records in alpine ski racing history. Shiffrin's winning margin of 0.56 seconds over silver medalist Wendy Holdener was the largest of her Killington victories — a statement of competitive authority on her de facto home race.

Shiffrin, who grew up in Vail, Colorado and trains at Vail Mountain as her primary home resort, has an emotional connection to the Killington event that she has described as unique in her career. Racing in the US, in front of American fans, in a sport where domestic crowds are rare — Killington's 30,000+ spectator attendance makes it unlike any other FIS World Cup event. Shiffrin typically dedicates time to fan interactions at Killington that she cannot offer at European venues.

Her first run was technically flawless — a near-record time on the Superstar trail's steep upper pitch that positioned her 0.40 seconds ahead of the field entering the second run. She skied the second run with the measured authority of a champion confirming an already-decided outcome.

**Nina O'Brien** (USA) competed in the slalom as a second discipline beyond her primary GS, finishing 20th — a respectable result as she continued rebuilding from her 2022 Olympic injury.

## Season Standings Impact
Shiffrin's Killington gold extended her lead in the FIS Alpine World Cup slalom standings and the overall standings. The result was her World Cup win of the 2023–2024 early season and placed her on pace to challenge her all-time win record further.
