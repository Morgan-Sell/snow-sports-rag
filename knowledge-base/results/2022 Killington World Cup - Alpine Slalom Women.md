# 2022 Killington World Cup — Alpine Ski Slalom Women Results

## Event Details
- **Date:** November 27, 2022
- **Venue:** Killington Resort, Vermont (Superstar trail)
- **Discipline:** Alpine Ski Racing — Slalom — Women
- **Conditions:** Firm and cold; excellent race-day conditions following two days of snowmaking and grooming

## Podium
1. **Mikaela Shiffrin** (USA) — 1:45.12 (combined two runs)
2. Katharina Truppe (AUT) — 1:45.98
3. Wendy Holdener (SUI) — 1:46.23

## US Team Performance
**Mikaela Shiffrin** (USA) delivered a wire-to-wire dominant performance at the Killington World Cup, extending her record for wins at the event. Coming just months after her bronze medal at the 2022 Beijing Winter Olympics in super-G, Shiffrin arrived at Killington with momentum from a strong early-season circuit.

Her first run on the Superstar course was the fastest of all competitors — an aggressive, technically precise performance that imposed her authority on the race from the outset. The second run was similarly quick and error-free. Her winning margin of 0.86 seconds was among the largest in Killington World Cup history.

The 30,000-strong crowd at Killington gave Shiffrin the most vocal home support she receives anywhere on the World Cup circuit. She acknowledged the crowd with a characteristic fist-pump at the finish and later described the Killington experience as her favorite of each season — not just for the win, but for the rare experience of racing with American fans creating a European-style atmosphere.

**Nina O'Brien** (USA) competed but was recovering from her 2022 Beijing leg fracture and did not finish. Her courageous return to World Cup racing at Killington just months after a career-threatening injury was acknowledged by Shiffrin and US Ski & Snowboard coaching staff.

## Season Standings Impact
Shiffrin's Killington gold confirmed her as the dominant early-season slalom leader. The result was one of 12+ World Cup victories she collected across the full 2022–2023 season — a record-breaking campaign.
