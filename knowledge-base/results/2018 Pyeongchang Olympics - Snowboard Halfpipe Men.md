# 2018 Pyeongchang Winter Olympics — Snowboard Halfpipe Men Results

## Event Details
- **Date:** February 14, 2018
- **Venue:** Phoenix Snow Park, Pyeongchang, South Korea
- **Discipline:** Snowboard Halfpipe — Men
- **Conditions:** Cold; overcast with stable winds; halfpipe maintained well for evening finals

## Podium
1. **Shaun White** (USA) — 97.75
2. Ayumu Hirano (JPN) — 95.25
3. Scott James (AUS) — 92.00

## US Team Performance
**Shaun White** (USA) delivered what may be the greatest single halfpipe run in Olympic history on his final attempt of the Pyeongchang finals. Going into his third and final run trailing Hirano, White landed the first-ever back-to-back Double Cork 1440s in Olympic competition — a pair of off-axis quadruple-rotation tricks that no competitor had attempted at the Olympics. His score of 97.75 vaulted him past Hirano to claim the gold medal, and with it, his third Olympic halfpipe title — the most of any snowboarder.

White, who had prepared for Pyeongchang with an intensive training block at Park City Mountain Resort and a private halfpipe session in New Zealand, described the moment as the culmination of four years of preparation following his disappointing 4th place at the 2014 Sochi Games. He was emotional at the finish — covering his face with the American flag — in a moment that became one of the most widely shared images of the 2018 Games.

White had competed at X Games Aspen weeks earlier, winning gold there as well, which had provided the competitive tune-up for the Olympic performance. He trained his run preparation at Park City Mountain Resort under US Ski & Snowboard coaching.

**Danny Davis** (USA) competed in the halfpipe, reaching the finals but finishing outside the podium. Davis, who trains at Mammoth Mountain, used the Olympic experience as a platform for the artistic riding philosophy that has defined his career.

## Historical Significance
White's three Olympic halfpipe gold medals (2006 Turin, 2010 Vancouver, 2018 Pyeongchang) are the most Olympic gold medals won by any snowboarder in history. His Pyeongchang performance — coming back from his 2014 failure — is the definitive redemption arc in snowboard Olympic history and one of the most watched Winter Olympic moments of the past decade.
