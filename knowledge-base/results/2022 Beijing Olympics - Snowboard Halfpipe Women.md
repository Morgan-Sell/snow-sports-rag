# 2022 Beijing Winter Olympics — Snowboard Halfpipe Women Results

## Event Details
- **Date:** February 10, 2022
- **Venue:** Genting Snow Park, Zhangjiakou, China
- **Discipline:** Snowboard Halfpipe — Women
- **Conditions:** Cold and clear; the Olympic halfpipe was built to 22-foot specifications on a north-facing slope

## Podium
1. **Chloe Kim** (USA) — 94.00
2. Queralt Castellet (ESP) — 90.25
3. Sena Tomita (JPN) — 88.25

## US Team Performance
**Chloe Kim** (USA) became the first woman to win back-to-back Olympic halfpipe gold medals, cementing her legacy as the greatest women's halfpipe snowboarder in history. Kim's gold-medal run at the Beijing Games was a masterpiece of technical precision and amplitude: she opened with a massive frontside 1080, transitioned to a Cab 1080, and closed with a frontside 900 performed at heights that no other competitor could approach. Her score of 94.00 was the highest of the Olympic finals and was awarded unanimously by the five-judge panel.

Kim, who trains primarily at Mammoth Mountain and uses Park City Mountain Resort for US Ski & Snowboard team camps, had prepared specifically for the Zhangjiakou halfpipe's characteristics — a longer than usual pipe with an aggressive pitch. Her preparation included early-January training at Park City and a final camp at Mammoth before Olympic travel.

Post-competition, Kim was emotional in her finish corral, saying she dedicated the gold to the US Ski & Snowboard coaching team and to her family. The victory was her second Olympic gold (after Pyeongchang 2018) and her most technically complete competition run.

**Hailey Langland** (USA) competed in the halfpipe as a secondary discipline, finishing outside the top eight but completing all of her runs — a strong result given the Olympic halfpipe pressure environment.

## Historical Significance
Kim's back-to-back gold is the defining achievement in women's halfpipe snowboard history. No woman had previously won consecutive Olympic halfpipe titles. Combined with her multiple FIS World Cup halfpipe globes and X Games records, the Beijing gold confirmed her as the most accomplished women's snowboarder of any generation.
