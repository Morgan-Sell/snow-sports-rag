# 2023 X Games Aspen — Snowboard Halfpipe Men Results

## Event Details
- **Date:** January 28, 2023
- **Venue:** Buttermilk Mountain, Aspen Snowmass, Colorado
- **Discipline:** Snowboard Halfpipe — Men
- **Conditions:** Clear skies; cold temperatures preserved pipe quality through finals

## Podium
1. Yuto Totsuka (JPN) — 95.00
2. **Ben Ferguson** (USA) — 92.25
3. Scotty James (AUS) — 90.75

## US Team Performance
**Ben Ferguson** (USA) claimed the silver medal with a technically progressive run featuring a Cab 1080, frontside 1260, and backside 900 performed at heights exceeding 18 feet above the pipe lip. Ferguson, who trains out of Mammoth Mountain for US Ski & Snowboard halfpipe camps, credited his winter training cycle at Mammoth for the consistent amplitude he maintained throughout the event. The silver was his best X Games result of the 2022–2023 season and placed him firmly in the conversation for the FIS World Cup halfpipe final standings.

**Danny Davis** (USA) competed and reached the final, finishing 6th. At 34, Davis remains one of the most stylistically distinctive pipe riders in the world. His presence in the X Games Aspen final field reflects his sustained relevance in elite halfpipe competition.

**Torin Yater-Wallace** (USA) finished 4th — narrowly missing the podium — with a creative run on his home mountain. Yater-Wallace, who grew up in Aspen and has trained at Buttermilk throughout his career, expressed pride in the result despite the near-miss.

## Season Standings Impact
Ben Ferguson's silver medal at X Games Aspen, combined with his FIS World Cup halfpipe podiums across the season, positioned the US men's halfpipe team as a genuine podium threat for the FIS World Cup Finals.
