# 2023 US Alpine Ski Championships — Slalom Women Results

## Event Details
- **Date:** April 2, 2023
- **Venue:** Sun Valley Resort, Idaho (Bald Mountain)
- **Discipline:** Alpine Ski Racing — Slalom — Women
- **Conditions:** Spring conditions; soft upper snowpack firming in the shadows on Baldy's course

## Podium
1. **Mikaela Shiffrin** (USA) — 1:48.92 (combined two runs)
2. AJ Hurt (USA) — 1:51.23
3. Katie Hensien (USA) — 1:52.04

## US Team Performance
**Mikaela Shiffrin** (USA) won the US Alpine Ski Championships slalom title at Sun Valley in dominant fashion — consistent with her comprehensive 2022–2023 World Cup season in which she won more World Cup races than any skier in history. Shiffrin's performance on Bald Mountain's slalom course was authoritative: she led after run one by a margin of 1.8 seconds — unusual even for domestic competition — and skied a controlled but still commanding second run to claim the title.

Shiffrin, who trains at Vail Mountain and grew up in Vail, Colorado, has won the US Alpine Championships slalom on multiple occasions at Sun Valley. The result is a formality given her World Cup dominance, but the Championships serve an important purpose as a platform for younger US slalom athletes to benchmark themselves against the best. Shiffrin engaged fully with the team culture at Sun Valley, spending time with developing US athletes during the Championships week.

**Nina O'Brien** (USA) competed in slalom as a second discipline, finishing outside the top five. O'Brien's participation in the Championships was itself significant given her 2022 Olympic injury; her continued return to competition across both GS and slalom disciplines was noted positively by US Ski & Snowboard coaching.

## Season Standings Impact
Shiffrin's US Championships title added the domestic crown to an unprecedented 2022–2023 season in which she broke Ingemar Stenmark's all-time World Cup wins record. The domestic title is the capstone of a year that will be remembered as one of the greatest seasons in alpine ski racing history.
