# 2022 Beijing Winter Olympics — Snowboard Slopestyle Women Results

## Event Details
- **Date:** February 6, 2022
- **Venue:** Genting Snow Park, Zhangjiakou, China
- **Discipline:** Snowboard Slopestyle — Women
- **Conditions:** Breezy but manageable; cold; course was large with three substantial jump features

## Podium
1. Zoi Sadowski-Synnott (NZL) — 92.88
2. **Julia Marino** (USA) — 87.68
3. Tess Coady (AUS) — 84.15

## US Team Performance
**Julia Marino** (USA) won the Olympic silver medal in snowboard slopestyle — the best individual result for a US woman in the discipline's Olympic history. Marino's silver-medal run was technically the most complete of her career to that point: her rail section, which coaches had identified as her competitive signature, earned the highest marks of any competitor in the event. She then landed a frontside 900 and a cab 1080 across the jump features to consolidate a comprehensive, well-balanced run. Her score of 87.68 placed her solidly on the podium behind the dominant Sadowski-Synnott.

Marino, who trains primarily at Mammoth Mountain, described the Olympic experience as transformational. She credited the US Ski & Snowboard slopestyle coaching staff and her Mammoth Mountain training environment for preparing her to perform on the Olympic stage without evident nerves.

**Jamie Anderson** (USA) — the two-time defending Olympic slopestyle champion — competed at Beijing but finished outside the podium. Anderson, whose home resort is Palisades Tahoe, acknowledged that the field's technical progression since her 2018 gold had been dramatic. Her participation was her third Olympic slopestyle appearance.

**Hailey Langland** (USA) competed in the slopestyle, finishing outside the top eight but completing all runs — a meaningful result for a young athlete at her second Olympics.

## Historical Significance
Julia Marino's silver placed her alongside Jamie Anderson as the only US women to win Olympic slopestyle medals. It marked a generational passing of the torch in the discipline, with Marino's technical railwork and big-jump execution setting a new standard for US women's slopestyle.
