# 2018 Pyeongchang Winter Olympics — Snowboard Slopestyle Men Results

## Event Details
- **Date:** February 11, 2018
- **Venue:** Phoenix Snow Park, Pyeongchang, South Korea
- **Discipline:** Snowboard Slopestyle — Men
- **Conditions:** Extremely windy during qualifying and early finals; conditions improved but remained challenging

## Podium
1. **Red Gerard** (USA) — 87.16
2. Max Parrot (CAN) — 86.00
3. Mark McMorris (CAN) — 85.20

## US Team Performance
**Red Gerard** (USA) won the Olympic gold medal in snowboard slopestyle in one of the most improbable and celebrated performances in Winter Olympic history. Gerard — 17 years old, making his Olympic debut, having overslept and almost missed competition, and having borrowed a teammate's jacket — fell on both his first and second runs before delivering a near-perfect final run with a score of 87.16. The run included a cab double cork 1080 on the first jump, a switch backside 1260 on the second, and a frontside 1260 on the third — all landed with Gerard's signature casual style.

Gerard, who grew up in Silverthorne, Colorado and had spent the bulk of his development years training at Breckenridge Ski Resort, became an overnight global celebrity. The story of the sleep-deprived teenager in a borrowed jacket winning gold captured international sports media for days. He dedicated the win to his family in Colorado and to his training team at Breckenridge.

The result was the first gold medal of the 2018 Pyeongchang Winter Olympics for the United States — a role Gerard wore with characteristic casualness.

## Historical Significance
Red Gerard's gold is one of the most memorable moments in snowboard Olympic history. His youth (youngest US male Winter Olympic gold medalist), the circumstances (last-run gold after two falls), and his personal story (Silverthorne local, Breckenridge product) combined to create a cultural moment that introduced millions of new viewers to competitive snowboarding. Gerard has since confirmed his status as a legitimate elite competitor rather than a one-hit wonder, with multiple World Cup podiums and X Games medals in the years following Pyeongchang.
