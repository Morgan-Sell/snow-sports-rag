# 2023 US Alpine Ski Championships — Giant Slalom Men Results

## Event Details
- **Date:** April 3, 2023
- **Venue:** Sun Valley Resort, Idaho (Bald Mountain)
- **Discipline:** Alpine Ski Racing — Giant Slalom — Men
- **Conditions:** Spring corn snow; Bald Mountain's north-facing pitch held firm conditions through both runs

## Podium
1. **Tommy Ford** (USA) — 2:28.34 (combined two runs)
2. Luke Winters (USA) — 2:29.87
3. River Radamus (USA) — 2:30.21

## US Team Performance
**Tommy Ford** (USA) claimed the US Alpine Championships giant slalom title at Sun Valley's Bald Mountain — confirming his position as the best active American men's GS ski racer. Ford's two-run performance was technically precise and aggressive through the GS gates, particularly impressive on Baldy's sustained fall-line terrain which rewards the sustained carving style Ford has developed in the Ted Ligety mold.

Ford, whose home resort is Mount Bachelor in Oregon and who trains with the US Ski & Snowboard alpine team program, described the US Championships as a meaningful domestic benchmark. While FIS ranking points are not at stake, the national title carries prestige and serves as a season-ending measure of US team competitiveness. Ford said his Killington World Cup result earlier in the season had been the strategic priority, but the US Championships gold at Sun Valley affirmed the consistency of his GS form.

**Ryan Cochran-Siegle** (USA) competed in the GS as a secondary discipline beyond his primary super-G, finishing 5th — a strong result in a discipline outside his specialty.

## Season Standings Impact
Tommy Ford's US Championships GS title confirmed him as the undisputed US national champion in the discipline, a title he has held consistently since the end of Ted Ligety's career. The result reinforced Ford's position as the top US men's GS contender for the 2026 Milan-Cortina Olympic cycle.
