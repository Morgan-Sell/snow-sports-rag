# 2020 X Games Aspen — Snowboard Halfpipe Women Results

## Event Details
- **Date:** January 24, 2020
- **Venue:** Buttermilk Mountain, Aspen Snowmass, Colorado
- **Discipline:** Snowboard Halfpipe — Women
- **Conditions:** Clear skies; Buttermilk's halfpipe held in excellent shape despite warm morning temperatures

## Podium
1. **Chloe Kim** (USA) — 95.50
2. Queralt Castellet (ESP) — 88.25
3. Maddie Mastro (USA) — 84.00

## US Team Performance
**Chloe Kim** (USA) returned to competition at X Games Aspen 2020 after her year away from competitive snowboarding to attend Princeton University. The return was met with enormous anticipation — and Kim delivered one of the most emphatic comeback performances in action sports history, posting a score of 95.50 that surpassed the previous X Games halfpipe women's record. Her winning run included back-to-back Cab 1080s and a frontside 1080 performed at heights that drew audible gasps from the Buttermilk crowd.

Kim, whose home resort and primary training base is Mammoth Mountain, had prepared for her return to competition with a targeted training block at Mammoth in the weeks prior to X Games. She later described the 2019 gap year as essential to her mental and emotional wellbeing, and the 2020 X Games gold as proof that the decision had made her a better athlete and person.

**Maddie Mastro** (USA) earned bronze — a breakthrough X Games result that confirmed her position as the third-ranked US halfpipe woman.

The 2020 X Games Aspen halfpipe was the first time since Kim's debut in 2015 that her absence had been felt on the X Games halfpipe circuit; her return made the event one of the most-watched in X Games Aspen history.

## Season Standings Impact
Kim's return at X Games Aspen 2020 re-established her as the clear frontrunner for the FIS Snowboard World Cup halfpipe globe — a title she would go on to win comprehensively across the 2019–2020 season. The result set the stage for her eventual dominant 2022 Olympic campaign.
