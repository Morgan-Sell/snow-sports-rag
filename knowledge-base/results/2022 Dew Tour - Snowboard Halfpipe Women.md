# 2022 Dew Tour — Snowboard Halfpipe Women Results

## Event Details
- **Date:** December 9, 2022
- **Venue:** Copper Mountain Resort, Colorado
- **Discipline:** Snowboard Halfpipe — Women
- **Conditions:** Early season; firm, well-shaped pipe; cold temperatures through finals

## Podium
1. **Chloe Kim** (USA) — 90.75
2. Queralt Castellet (ESP) — 86.50
3. **Hailey Langland** (USA) — 79.25

## US Team Performance
**Chloe Kim** (USA) opened the 2022–2023 FIS Snowboard World Cup season with a dominant Dew Tour halfpipe gold at Copper Mountain Resort — the event's permanent home venue. Kim's winning run featured back-to-back Cab 1080s followed by a massive frontside 900, with amplitude consistently exceeding 20 feet above the pipe lip. The score of 90.75 was exceptional for an early-season result and signaled to rivals that Kim's Olympic form had carried seamlessly into the new campaign.

Kim trains primarily at Mammoth Mountain, where she and other US halfpipe team members complete their pre-season pipe preparation before the competition season opens. Copper Mountain's December Dew Tour is typically her first official competitive appearance of the year, and her Dew Tour record — multiple golds across consecutive seasons — reflects her comfort with the venue and its specific halfpipe build.

**Hailey Langland** (USA) earned bronze in halfpipe — her best early-season pipe result — with a run that showcased improving amplitude and technical variety. Langland, who trains at Mammoth Mountain alongside Kim under the US Ski & Snowboard halfpipe program, credited their shared training environment for accelerating her halfpipe development.

## Season Standings Impact
As a full FIS Snowboard World Cup event, the Dew Tour awarded ranking points that kicked off the 2022–2023 Olympic qualifying season standings. Kim's gold placed her atop the early World Cup halfpipe standings — a position she would maintain through the entire season to claim the globe.
