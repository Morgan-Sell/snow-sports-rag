# 2022 Beijing Winter Olympics — Freeski Slopestyle Men Results

## Event Details
- **Date:** February 16, 2022
- **Venue:** Genting Snow Park, Zhangjiakou, China
- **Discipline:** Freeski Slopestyle — Men
- **Conditions:** Cold and clear; the Olympic slopestyle course featured three large jump features and six rail elements

## Podium
1. Birk Ruud (NOR) — 90.00
2. Colby Stevenson (USA) — 86.75
3. Nico Porteous (NZL) — 85.00

## US Team Performance
**Colby Stevenson** (USA) delivered a remarkable silver medal run at the Beijing Winter Olympics — one of the most emotional stories of the 2022 Games. Stevenson had survived a near-fatal car crash in 2016, suffering a traumatic brain injury that left him with lasting physical effects including impaired balance. His Olympic silver came six years after that accident, representing one of the most unlikely comeback stories in the history of competitive skiing.

**Alex Hall** (USA) finished 4th — just off the podium — with a run that featured some of the most stylistically creative trick combinations in the field. Hall, who trains at Park City Mountain Resort and grew up there, delivered a run that earned the highest style scores of any competitor but received lower difficulty marks than the podium athletes, resulting in the narrow miss. His 4th place at Beijing was a heartbreaking result that the US freeski community rallied around, with coaches and competitors alike expressing confidence in Hall's continued dominance at the World Cup level.

**Nick Goepper** (USA) competed in the Beijing slopestyle, finishing 6th — a solid Olympic performance that demonstrated his continued relevance more than eight years after his first Olympic medal at Sochi 2014. Goepper, whose home resort is Steamboat Springs, acknowledged that his Olympic story — from 2014 silver to personal struggles to 2022 Olympic return — had given the sport a narrative of resilience and recovery.

## Historical Significance
The US men's freeski slopestyle program placed three athletes in the top-six at the 2022 Beijing Olympics — a display of national team depth that no other country could match. Alex Hall's near-miss at 4th place motivated his dominant 2022–2023 World Cup and X Games season, in which he won the FIS World Championships and multiple X Games golds.
