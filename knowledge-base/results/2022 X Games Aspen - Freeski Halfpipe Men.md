# 2022 X Games Aspen — Freeski Halfpipe Men Results

## Event Details
- **Date:** January 22, 2022
- **Venue:** Buttermilk Mountain, Aspen Snowmass, Colorado
- **Discipline:** Freeski Halfpipe — Men
- **Conditions:** Cold; firm pipe surface; held without spectators due to COVID protocols

## Podium
1. David Wise (USA) — 93.00
2. **Ben Ferguson** (USA) — 90.50
3. **Torin Yater-Wallace** (USA) — 88.25

## US Team Performance
The US men's freeski halfpipe team delivered a remarkable sweep of the podium at X Games Aspen 2022.

**David Wise** (USA) won the gold with a run that included a massive rightside 1440 as its centerpiece, performed with exceptional amplitude and clean execution.

**Ben Ferguson** (USA) claimed silver with his most polished halfpipe run of the season, featuring Cab 1080 and frontside 1080 variations executed at consistently high amplitude. Ferguson, who trains seasonally at Mammoth Mountain, credited the quality of the halfpipe build at Buttermilk for enabling the amplitude levels achieved by all competitors.

**Torin Yater-Wallace** (USA) earned bronze at his home mountain — Buttermilk, within the Aspen Snowmass complex where he grew up. A bronze on the mountain where he learned to ski was personally meaningful to Yater-Wallace, who described the result as "coming home." He trains partly at Aspen Snowmass and partly with the US team at Mammoth Mountain and Park City Mountain Resort.

## Season Standings Impact
The US podium sweep at X Games Aspen 2022 announced the US men's freeski halfpipe program as the strongest national team in the discipline heading into the 2022 Beijing Winter Olympics.
