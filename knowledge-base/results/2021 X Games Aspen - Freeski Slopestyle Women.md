# 2021 X Games Aspen — Freeski Slopestyle Women Results

## Event Details
- **Date:** January 29, 2021
- **Venue:** Buttermilk Mountain, Aspen Snowmass, Colorado
- **Discipline:** Freeski Slopestyle — Women
- **Conditions:** X Games Aspen 2021 held without spectators due to COVID-19 protocols; cold and clear

## Podium
1. **Maggie Voisin** (USA) — 86.00
2. **Hailey Langland** (USA) — 83.75
3. Mathilde Gremaud (SUI) — 81.50

## US Team Performance
**Maggie Voisin** (USA) won X Games Aspen freeski slopestyle gold — her first X Games gold medal — with a technically decisive run that earned the highest rail section score in the women's field. Voisin, who grew up in Whitefish, Montana and trained in the Whitefish Mountain Resort ecosystem before moving into the US Ski & Snowboard slopestyle program, delivered a run that reflected the creative, precise style she has developed across more than five seasons of elite competition.

Voisin's gold at Buttermilk Mountain was particularly meaningful given the lack of spectators. She described competing on the Buttermilk course in silence as strange but motivating — the performance was for the sport itself.

**Hailey Langland** (USA) claimed silver — her best X Games freeski slopestyle result — with a powerful run that showcased her developing big-air jump capabilities. Langland, who trains at Mammoth Mountain, has benefited from the US Ski & Snowboard multi-discipline program that allows her to develop slopestyle and halfpipe skills simultaneously.

## Season Standings Impact
Voisin's X Games gold, combined with FIS World Cup podiums across the 2020–2021 season, positioned her as the leading US women's freeski slopestyle competitor and a legitimate World Cup globe contender entering the Olympic qualifying cycle.
